<p style="margin:0in 0in .0001pt;"><font size="2" face="Times New Roman" style="font-size:10.0pt;">ITEM 8.01&#160;&#160;&#160;&#160;&#160;&#160;&#160; Other Events.</font></p>

<p style="margin:0in 0in .0001pt;"><font size="2" face="Times New Roman" style="font-size:10.0pt;">&nbsp;</font></p>

<p style="margin:0in 0in .0001pt;"><font size="2" face="Times New Roman" style="font-size:10.0pt;">&#160;&#160;&#160;&#160;&#160;&#160;&#160;&#160;&#160;&#160;&#160;&#160;&#160;&#160;&#160; On
December 21, 2004, Tenet Healthcare Corporation issued a press release
announcing that it had reached an agreement in principle with lawyers
representing former cardiac care patients at Redding Medical Center to settle
substantially all patient litigation against the company and its subsidiaries
arising out of allegations that unnecessary medical procedures were performed
at the hospital before November 2002.&#160;
Tenet also announced that the Redding settlement would cause the company
to breach certain financial covenants in its existing bank credit line, which
is currently undrawn.&#160; Therefore, the
company said it is in its best interests to terminate the credit line before
the end of this year and negotiate a new bank credit line early next year.&#160; The press release is attached as Exhibit
99.1.</font></p>

<p align="center" style="color:windowtext;margin:0in 0in .0001pt;text-align:center;"><b><font size="2" face="Times New Roman" style="font-size:10.0pt;font-weight:bold;">Tenet
Reaches Agreement to Settle Redding Patient Litigation</font></b></p>

<p align="center" style="color:windowtext;margin:0in 0in .0001pt;text-align:center;"><font size="2" face="Times New Roman" style="font-size:10.0pt;">&nbsp;</font></p>

<p style="color:windowtext;margin:0in 0in 6.0pt;"><b><font size="2" face="Times New Roman" style="font-size:10.0pt;font-weight:bold;">&#160;&#160;&#160;&#160;&#160;&#160;&#160;&#160;&#160;&#160;&#160;&#160;&#160;&#160;&#160; DEC. 21, 2004 &#151; </font></b><font size="2" style="font-size:10.0pt;">Tenet
Healthcare Corporation (NYSE: THC) announced today that it has reached an
agreement in principle with lawyers representing former cardiac care patients
at Redding Medical Center to settle substantially all patient litigation
against the company and its subsidiaries arising out of allegations that
unnecessary medical procedures were performed at the hospital before November
2002.</font></p>

<p style="color:windowtext;margin:0in 0in 6.0pt;"><font size="2" face="Times New Roman" style="font-size:10.0pt;">&#160;&#160;&#160;&#160;&#160;&#160;&#160;&#160;&#160;&#160;&#160;&#160;&#160;&#160;&#160; Under the agreement, Tenet will establish
a settlement fund by Dec. 31, 2004, of $395 million to be allocated among more
than 750 plaintiffs who had filed civil lawsuits. The cases arose from
allegations that certain doctors had performed unnecessary cardiac
catheterizations and bypass surgeries while practicing at Redding Medical
Center in Redding, Calif.&#160; The litigation
against those physicians is not part of this settlement.</font></p>

<p style="color:windowtext;margin:0in 0in 6.0pt;"><font size="2" face="Times New Roman" style="font-size:10.0pt;">&#160;&#160;&#160;&#160;&#160;&#160;&#160;&#160;&#160;&#160;&#160;&#160;&#160;&#160;&#160; The settlement agreement is
subject to customary procedural requirements of the California state court and
ratification by substantially all the individual plaintiffs.</font></p>

<p style="color:windowtext;margin:0in 0in 6.0pt;"><font size="2" face="Times New Roman" style="font-size:10.0pt;">&#160;&#160;&#160;&#160;&#160;&#160;&#160;&#160;&#160;&#160;&#160;&#160;&#160;&#160;&#160; &#147;We believe this settlement is
the fair and honorable way to conclude this very sad chapter,&#148; said Trevor
Fetter, president and chief executive officer.&#160;
&#147;It would likely have taken multiple trials and many years to assess
liability in these cases.&#160; By settling
all the cases at once, we put this matter behind both </font></p>

<p style="color:windowtext;margin:0in 0in 6.0pt;"><font size="2" face="Times New Roman" style="font-size:10.0pt;">&#160;&#160;&#160;&#160;&#160;&#160;&#160;&#160;&#160;&#160;&#160;&#160;&#160;&#160;&#160; Fetter added, &#147;We are building a

<p style="color:windowtext;margin:0in 0in 6.0pt;"><font size="2" face="Times New Roman" style="font-size:10.0pt;">&#160;&#160;&#160;&#160;&#160;&#160;&#160;&#160;&#160;&#160;&#160;&#160;&#160;&#160;&#160; E. Peter Urbanowicz, Tenet&#146;s
general counsel, said, &#147;With this settlement, we take a significant step
forward in resolving the serious legal challenges that Tenet has faced as a
result of events that took place before November 2002.&#160; We are seriously focused on bringing to a
satisfactory conclusion all our remaining litigation and investigations, and
this settlement is strong evidence of our progress.&#148;</font></p>

<p style="color:windowtext;margin:0in 0in 6.0pt;"><font size="2" face="Times New Roman" style="font-size:10.0pt;">&#160;&#160;&#160;&#160;&#160;&#160;&#160;&#160;&#160;&#160;&#160;&#160;&#160;&#160;&#160; Law firms representing the
plaintiffs include Reiner, Simpson, Timmons &amp; Slaughter in Redding; Barr
&amp; Mudford in Redding; Moriarty Leyendecker in Houston; Hackerman Frankel in
Houston; and Gillin, Jacobson, Ellis &amp; Larson in Orinda, Calif.</font></p>

<p style="color:windowtext;margin:0in 0in 6.0pt;"><font size="2" face="Times New Roman" style="font-size:10.0pt;">&#160;&#160;&#160;&#160;&#160;&#160;&#160;&#160;&#160;&#160;&#160;&#160;&#160;&#160;&#160; Attorneys from those firms
representing former patients said in a joint statement, &#147;The former patients of
Redding Medical Center can never get back what they had, but this settlement
provides a measure of justice and closure.&#160;
All involved in this tragedy can move forward and begin the healing
process.&#160; This has been a long and
difficult road for our clients.&#160; We are
extremely proud of their courage and are pleased that these remarkable people
are being compensated for the acts committed by these surgeons.&#148;</font></p>

<p style="color:windowtext;margin:0in 0in 6.0pt;"><font size="2" face="Times New Roman" style="font-size:10.0pt;">&#160;&#160;&#160;&#160;&#160;&#160;&#160;&#160;&#160;&#160;&#160;&#160;&#160;&#160;&#160; Tenet said it has general
liability insurance.&#160; It noted, however,
that its insurance carriers thus far have raised objections to coverage under
the policies at issue.&#160; The company said
it intends to pursue vigorously its coverage rights.</font></p>

<p style="color:windowtext;margin:0in 0in 6.0pt;"><font size="2" face="Times New Roman" style="font-size:10.0pt;">&#160;&#160;&#160;&#160;&#160;&#160;&#160;&#160;&#160;&#160;&#160;&#160;&#160;&#160;&#160; &#147;In cases of this magnitude, it
is a common practice for insurance companies to dispute coverage,&#148; Urbanowicz
said.</font></p>

settlement would cause the company to breach certain financial covenants in its
existing bank credit line, which is currently undrawn.&#160; Therefore, the company said it is </font></p>

<p style="color:windowtext;margin:0in 0in 6.0pt;"><font size="2" face="Times New Roman" style="font-size:10.0pt;">&#160;&#160;&#160;&#160;&#160;&#160;&#160;&#160;&#160;&#160;&#160;&#160;&#160;&#160;&#160; In August 2003, Tenet agreed to

the Redding matters.&#160; Last June, the
company sold substantially all the assets of Redding Medical Center to an
affiliate of Hospital Partners of America Inc.</font></p>

<p style="color:windowtext;margin:0in 0in 6.0pt;"><font size="2" face="Times New Roman" style="font-size:10.0pt;">&#160;&#160;&#160;&#160;&#160;&#160;&#160;&#160;&#160;&#160;&#160;&#160;&#160;&#160;&#160; Tenet Healthcare Corporation,

quality and service.&nbsp; Tenet can be found on the World Wide Web at www.tenethealth.com.</font></p>
