<p align="left" style="margin:0in 0in .0001pt;text-align:left;text-indent:.5in;"><font size="2" face="Times New Roman" style="font-size:10.0pt;">In fiscal 2004, net
revenues declined approximately 3% to $8,533,000 from $8,758,000 in fiscal
2003.&#160; The decrease ($225,000) in net
revenues was principally attributed to lower sales volume associated with the
Lee&#174; Lip-Ex&#174; lip balm category ($111,000), various personal care items
($207,000) and One Step at a Time&#174; ($169,000).&#160;
In addition, the Company experienced lower volume from a number of its
many over-the-counter items ($367,000).&#160;
The Company believes that these declines are to be expected from old established
brands that are not being supported by advertising or promotion.&#160; Somewhat offsetting these decreases in net
revenues were increases in the private label ($231,000) segment along with the
added sales of a newly acquired brand ($193,000).&#160; Also, the Company reported increases in
several over-the-counter items ($248,000).&#160;
The Company&#146;s sales returns decreased approximately $28,000 or 3% when
comparing fiscal years 2004 and 2003.&#160;
The Company experienced fewer deletions of their SKUs (stock keeping
units) at the retail store level during fiscal 2004.&#160; Company efforts have continued in the
direction of expansion into the private label business segment.</font></p>

$1,229,000, or 30 cents per share, during fiscal year 2004.&#160; This compares to a net loss of $1,104,000, or
27 cents per share, in fiscal year 2003.&#160;
Contributing to the loss during fiscal year 2004 was an increase in the
allowance for obsolete inventory ($456,000).</font></p>

<p align="left" style="margin:0in 0in .0001pt;text-align:left;text-indent:27.0pt;"><font size="2" face="Times New Roman" style="font-size:10.0pt;">&nbsp;</font></p>

<p align="left" style="margin:0in 0in .0001pt;text-align:left;text-indent:.5in;"><font size="2" face="Times New Roman" style="font-size:10.0pt;">The Company reported a decrease of
$154,000 in other income when comparing fiscal years 2004 and 2003.&#160; The decrease was attributed to lower realized
gains from the sale of four of the Company&#146;s product lines ($191,000), a
decrease in the Company&#146;s portion of the net profits from its investment of a
product line with Monticello Drug Company ($153,000) and a decrease of property
tax refunds recorded as other income ($15,000).&#160;
Offsetting these decreases was an increase of $205,000 from the Company&#146;s
distribution agreement with Monticello Drug Company.</font></p>
