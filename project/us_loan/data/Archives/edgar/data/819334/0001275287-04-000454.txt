<font size="2" face="Times New Roman"><b>Item 2.02&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Results of Operations and Financial Condition</b></font></p>
<p>
<font size="2" face="Times New Roman">&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;On December 21, 2004, CAM Commerce Solutions, Inc released a revised balance sheet (see exhibit 99.1) as of September 30, 2004. The balance sheet was previously released in a press release and furnished on Form 8-K on November 15, 2004.&#160; We revised the balance sheet to include an increase in deferred tax assets in the amount of $2.4 million with a related increase to capital in excess of par value for the same amount. This revision was based on further analysis of the deferred tax assets and related reduction of the valuation allowance for deferred taxes. We utilize the liability method of accounting for income taxes as set forth in Statement of Financial Accounting Standard No. 109, &#147;Accounting for Income Taxes&#148;. The valuation allowance was reversed based on our belief that sufficient future income from operations is expected to be able to recognize the net deferred tax assets. In
assessing the need for a valuation allowance, we consider all positive and negative evidence, including projected future taxable income, and recent financial performance.</font></p>

<p  align=right><font size="2" face="Times New Roman"><b>Exhibit 99.1</b></font></p>

<font size="2" face="Times New Roman"><b>CAM Releases Revised September 30, 2004 Balance Sheet</b></font></p>
<p  align="center">
<font size="2" face="Times New Roman">Increase in Deferred Net Tax Assets Results in Increased Capital, No Change to Reported Earnings Results</font></p>
<p>
<font size="2" face="Times New Roman">&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;FOUNTAIN VALLEY, Calif., Dec. 21 /PRNewswire-FirstCall/ -- CAM Commerce Solutions, Inc. (Nasdaq: CADA) announced today that it concluded this week that its balance sheet at September 30, 2004 that was previously released on November 15, 2004 understated its net deferred tax assets by $2.4 million.&#160; It has revised its balance sheet to include an increase in net deferred tax assets of $2.4 million, which has resulted in an increase in capital in excess of par value for the same amount.&#160; Upon further analysis by the company of its financial performance for fiscal year 2004, its projected future taxable income, and other factors, the valuation allowance was reversed based on the company&#146;s belief that it will earn sufficient future income from operations to be able to utilize its deferred tax assets.&#160; This reversal resulted in the increase in net deferred tax assets from zero to $2.4
million.</font></p>
<p>
<font size="2" face="Times New Roman">&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;The company utilizes the liability method of accounting for income taxes as set forth in Statement of Financial Accounting Standard No. 109, &#147;Accounting for Income Taxes&#148;.&#160; Deferred taxes are determined based on the difference between financial statement and tax bases of assets and liabilities using federal and state tax rates.&#160; Deferred tax assets include accruals not currently deductible for taxes, book depreciation in excess of tax depreciation, tax credit carryforwards, and net operating loss carryforwards.&#160; Under SFAS No. 109, when a company believes that it may not have sufficient income to be able to utilize its deferred tax assets, a valuation allowance is recorded.&#160; The difference between the valuation allowance and the deferred tax assets is the amount of net deferred tax assets.&#160; Prior to today&#146;s revision of the company&#146;s net deferred tax assets, the
company&#146;s valuation allowance for deferred tax assets at fiscal year end 2002 and 2003 had reduced its net deferred tax assets to zero.</font></p>

<p><font size="2" face="Times New Roman">&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;About CAM Commerce Solutions<br> &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;CAM Commerce Solutions, Inc. provides total commerce solutions for traditional and web retailers that are based on the Company&#146;s open architecture software products for inventory management, point of sale, sales transaction processing, accounting, and payment processing.&#160; These solutions often include hardware, installation, training, service, and payment processing services provided by the Company.&#160; You can visit CAM Commerce Solutions at www.camcommerce.com.</font></p>
