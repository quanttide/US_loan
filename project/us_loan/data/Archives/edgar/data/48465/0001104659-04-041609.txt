<p style="margin:0in 0in .0001pt;"><font size="2" face="Times New Roman" style="font-size:10.0pt;">&nbsp;</font></p>

<p style="margin:0in 0in .0001pt;"><b><font size="2" face="Times New Roman" style="font-size:10.0pt;font-weight:bold;">Item 1.01 <u>Entry into a Material Definitive
Agreement</u></font></b></p>

<p style="margin:0in 0in .0001pt;"><font size="2" face="Times New Roman" style="font-size:10.0pt;">&nbsp;</font></p>

<p style="font-size:10.0pt;margin:0in 0in .0001pt;"><font size="2" face="Times New Roman" style="font-size:10.0pt;">On December 29, 2004,
Hormel Foods Corporation (&#147;Hormel&#148;) entered into a definitive Stock Purchase
Agreement </font>with Joseph D.
Clougherty, Anthony P. Clougherty, Bernard J. Clougherty, Kathleen C. Regan,
the Bernard J. Clougherty Annuity Trust, the Joseph D. Clougherty Annuity
Trust, the Anthony P. Clougherty Annuity Trust and the Kathleen C. Regan
Annuity Trust (collectively, the &#147;<u>Sellers</u>&#148; and each a &#147;<u>Seller</u>&#148;)
and Patrick F. Collins (the &#147;<u>Sellers&#146;</u><b><font style="font-weight:bold;"> </font></b><u>Representative</u>&#148;)&#160; for the acquisition of all of the
stock of Clougherty Packing Company (Clougherty), a privately held Southern
California pork processor and creator of the &#147;Farmer John&#148; brand of pork
products popular throughout the Southwestern United States.&#160; The transaction closed on December 29, 2004.</p>

<p style="margin:0in 0in .0001pt;"><font size="2" face="Times New Roman" style="font-size:10.0pt;">&nbsp;</font></p>

<p style="margin:0in 0in .0001pt;"><font size="2" face="Times New Roman" style="font-size:10.0pt;">The
purchase price for all of the stock was approximately $186 million, and was
paid in cash on December 30, 2004.&#160; The
purchase price is subject to a post-closing working capital and EBITDA
adjustment to be completed within four months after closing.&#160;&#160; In addition to the stock purchase, Hormel
also agreed to purchase from the Sellers, subject to certain conditions, certain
live growing operations located in California for up to an additional $10
million. &#160;The parties have also agreed
that the Sellers shall retain the responsibility for remediating certain
pre-existing environmental conditions on the acquired properties following the
closing.</font></p>

<p style="margin:0in 0in .0001pt;"><font size="2" face="Times New Roman" style="font-size:10.0pt;">&nbsp;</font></p>

<p style="margin:0in 0in .0001pt;"><font size="2" face="Times New Roman" style="font-size:10.0pt;">Hormel
intends to file the Agreement with its next quarterly report on Form 10-Q.</font></p>

<p style="margin:0in 0in .0001pt;"><font size="2" face="Times New Roman" style="font-size:10.0pt;">&nbsp;</font></p>

<p style="margin:0in 0in .0001pt;"><font size="2" face="Times New Roman" style="font-size:10.0pt;">&nbsp;</font></p>

<p align="center" style="margin:0in 0in .0001pt;text-align:center;"><b><font size="2" face="Times New Roman" style="font-size:10.0pt;font-weight:bold;">Hormel Foods Acquires Clougherty Packing Company</font></b></p>

<p style="margin:0in 0in .0001pt;"><font size="2" face="Times New Roman" style="font-size:10.0pt;">&nbsp;</font></p>

<p style="margin:0in 0in .0001pt;"><font size="2" face="Times New Roman" style="font-size:10.0pt;">AUSTIN, MINN. (Dec. 30,
2004) &#150; Hormel Foods Corporation (NYSE: HRL) today announced that the company
has acquired, for the purchase price of approximately $186 million, all of the
stock of Clougherty Packing Company (Clougherty), a privately-held Southern
California pork processor and creator of the <i><font style="font-style:italic;">Farmer John</font></i>
brand of pork products popular through the Southwestern United States.</font></p>

<p style="margin:0in 0in .0001pt;"><font size="2" face="Times New Roman" style="font-size:10.0pt;">&nbsp;</font></p>

<p style="margin:0in 0in .0001pt;"><font size="2" face="Times New Roman" style="font-size:10.0pt;">&#147;Clougherty has built a
very strong business in the Southwest, and projects sales of $420 million in
2004.&#160; We expect it to be immediately
accretive to our Refrigerated Foods business segment,&#148; said Joel W. Johnson,
chairman of the board and chief executive officer.</font></p>

<p style="margin:0in 0in .0001pt;"><font size="2" face="Times New Roman" style="font-size:10.0pt;">&nbsp;</font></p>

<p style="margin:0in 0in .0001pt;"><font size="2" face="Times New Roman" style="font-size:10.0pt;">&#147;This transaction helps
strengthen our presence in the Southwest, and is a great strategic fit for
Hormel Foods,&#148; Johnson added.&#160; &#147;The <i><font style="font-style:italic;">Farmer John</font></i> brand enjoys the top market position in Southern
California in bacon, breakfast sausage and sliced hams, and has very strong
positions in hot dogs, fully cooked bacon and spiral sliced hams.&#160; Various <i><font style="font-style:italic;">Farmer John</font></i>
brand products also lend themselves to incremental sales through Hormel Foods&#146;
foodservice channels.</font></p>

<p style="margin:0in 0in .0001pt;"><font size="2" face="Times New Roman" style="font-size:10.0pt;">&nbsp;</font></p>

<p style="margin:0in 0in .0001pt;"><font size="2" face="Times New Roman" style="font-size:10.0pt;">&#147;The <i><font style="font-style:italic;">Farmer John</font></i>
brand has a broad consumer franchise in the Southwest, including a strong
following with Hispanic consumers.&#160; This
makes <i><font style="font-style:italic;">Farmer John</font></i> a valuable addition to
Hormel Foods&#146; existing <i><font style="font-style:italic;">Herdez, Do&#241;a Mar&#237;a</font></i>
and <i><font style="font-style:italic;">B&#250;falo</font></i> product families,&#148; Johnson said.</font></p>

<p style="margin:0in 0in .0001pt;"><font size="2" face="Times New Roman" style="font-size:10.0pt;">&nbsp;</font></p>

<p style="margin:0in 0in .0001pt;"><font size="2" face="Times New Roman" style="font-size:10.0pt;">Clougherty operates a
facility in Vernon, Calif., which should increase Hormel Foods&#146; production of
further-processed foods to meet our growing demand.</font></p>

<p style="margin:0in 0in .0001pt;"><font size="2" face="Times New Roman" style="font-size:10.0pt;">&nbsp;</font></p>

<p style="margin:0in 0in .0001pt;"><font size="2" face="Times New Roman" style="font-size:10.0pt;">About Hormel Foods
Corporation</font></p>

<p style="margin:0in 0in .0001pt;"><font size="2" face="Times New Roman" style="font-size:10.0pt;">&nbsp;</font></p>

<p style="margin:0in 0in .0001pt;"><font size="2" face="Times New Roman" style="font-size:10.0pt;">Hormel Foods Corporation,
based in Austin, Minn., is a multinational manufacturer and marketer of
consumer-branded food and meat products, many of which are among the best known
and trusted in the food industry.&#160; The
company leverages its extensive expertise, innovation and high competencies in
pork and turkey processing and marketing to bring quality, value-added brands
to the global marketplace.&#160; The company
enjoys a strong reputation among consumers, retail grocers, and foodservice and
industrial customers for products highly regarded for quality, taste,
nutrition, convenience and value.&#160; For
