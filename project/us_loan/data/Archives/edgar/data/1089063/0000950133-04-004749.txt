<P align="left" style="font-size: 10pt">ITEM 1.01 ENTRY INTO A MATERIAL DEFINITIVE AGREEMENT



<P align="left" style="font-size: 10pt">&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;In connection with the expiration, on December&nbsp;22, 2004, of the consent solicitation commenced
on December&nbsp;9, 2004 by Dick&#146;s Sporting Goods, Inc. (the &#147;Company&#148;) (NYSE: DKS) to attain consents
to amend the indenture under which the Company&#146;s $255,085,000 aggregate principal amount at
maturity Senior Convertible Notes due 2024 (the &#147;Notes&#148;) were issued (the &#147;Indenture&#148;), the Company
and Wachovia Bank, National Association (the &#147;Trustee&#148;) entered into the Supplemental Indenture
(the &#147;Supplemental Indenture&#148;), dated as of December&nbsp;22, 2004, among the Company and the Trustee,
which is binding on all holders of the Notes. The Supplemental Indenture removed the restriction
which prohibited the Company from paying cash upon the conversion of any Note if there has occurred
and is continuing an Event of Default under the Indenture.


<P align="left" style="font-size: 10pt">&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;On the date hereof, the Company also obtained a consent (the &#147;Credit Consent&#148;) to
the Company&#146;s Second Amended and Restated Credit Agreement (the &#147;Credit Agreement&#148;), executed among
the Company, certain lenders and General Electric Capital Corporation as agent.


<P align="left" style="font-size: 10pt">&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;The Credit Agreement, which provides for a revolving credit facility in an aggregate
outstanding principal amount of up to $350&nbsp;million, including up to $75&nbsp;million in the form of
letters of credit, contains two negative covenants which restrict the Company&#146;s ability to execute
the Supplemental Indenture and issue consent payments to holders of the Notes who consented to the
amendment. The Company executed the Credit Consent to allow the execution of the Supplemental
Indenture and payment to the holders of the Notes, as provided for in the solicitation of consents,
under the terms of the Credit Agreement.


<P align="left" style="font-size: 10pt">&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;The summary of the Supplemental Indenture and Credit Consent in this Current Report on Form
8-K are qualified in their entirety to the full text of the Supplemental Indenture and Credit
Consent attached hereto as Exhibit&nbsp;10.1 and Exhibit&nbsp;10.2, respectively.


<P align="left" style="font-size: 10pt">ITEM 8.01. OTHER EVENTS



<P align="left" style="font-size: 10pt">&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;On December&nbsp;23, 2004, the Company issued a press release announcing that it had successfully
completed a solicitation of consents to amend the Indenture under which the Company&#146;s Notes were
issued. The press release announcing the expiration of the Company&#146;s consent solicitation and
execution of the Supplemental Indenture is attached as Exhibit&nbsp;99.1 and is incorporated herein by
reference.

<P align="left" style="font-size: 10pt">&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;<B>2.01 Amendment to Section&nbsp;4.1 and Section&nbsp;4.2 of the Indenture &#151; Conversion Privilege and
Conversion Procedure.</B>


<P align="left" style="font-size: 10pt">&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Sections&nbsp;4.1(c) of the Indenture has been stricken in its entirety, and Section&nbsp;4.1(b) and
Section&nbsp;4.2(b) of the Indenture are hereby amended and restated in its entirety as follows:


<P align="left" style="font-size: 10pt">&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;<B>SECTION 4.1. CONVERSION PRIVILEGE.</B>


<P align="left" style="font-size: 10pt">&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;(b)&nbsp;The Holders&#146; rights to convert Securities into shares of Common Stock is subject to
the Company&#146;s obligation to pay each such Holder the amount of cash set forth in this paragraph, in
lieu of delivering some or all of such shares of Common Stock. The Company will pay to a Holder
cash for each $1,000 Principal Amount at Maturity of a Security surrendered for conversion in an
amount equal to the lesser of (1)&nbsp;the Accreted Principal Amount of such Security surrendered by
such Holder on the Conversion Date and (2)&nbsp;the product of (i)&nbsp;the number of shares of Common Stock
into which such Security otherwise would have been converted if no cash payment were made by the
Company, multiplied by (ii)&nbsp;the Average Sale Price of a share of Common Stock. The Company shall
determine that number of shares (the &#147;Balance Shares&#148;) which equals the number of shares of Common
Stock into which such Security of such Holder otherwise would be converted if no cash payment were
made by the Company, reduced, but not below zero, by an amount equal to the Accreted Principal
Amount of such Security on the Conversion Date, divided by the Average Sale Price of a share of
Common Stock. If the number of Balance Shares is zero, a Holder will not be entitled to any further
payment of cash or shares upon conversion. If the number of Balance Shares is greater than zero,
the Company will have the option to deliver cash or a combination of cash and shares of Common
Stock for the Balance Shares by electing for each full Balance Share for which the Company has
chosen to deliver cash to pay cash in an amount equal to the Average Sale Price of a share of
Common Stock.


<P align="left" style="font-size: 10pt">&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;<B>SECTION 4.2. CONVERSION PROCEDURE.</B>


<P align="left" style="font-size: 10pt">&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;(b)&nbsp;The Company shall deliver to the Holder surrendering such Security the amount of cash
payable with respect to such Security no later than the second Business Day following the
determination of the applicable Average Sale Price, together with a certificate for the number of
full shares of Common Stock, if any, deliverable upon the conversion (to the extent certificated)
and cash in lieu of any fractional share determined pursuant to Section&nbsp;4.3 hereof. If shares of
Common Stock are delivered as consideration, then the Person in whose name the certificate
representing such shares is registered shall be treated as a stockholder of record of the Company
on and after the Conversion Date; provided, however, that no surrender of a Security on any date
when the stock transfer books of the Company shall be closed shall be effective to constitute the
Person or Persons entitled to receive the shares of Common Stock upon such conversion as the record
holder or holders of such shares of Common Stock on such date, but such surrender shall be
effective to constitute the Person or Persons entitled to receive such shares of Common Stock as
the record holder or holders thereof for all purposes at the close of business on the next
succeeding day on which such stock transfer books are open; such conversion shall be at the
Conversion Rate in effect on the Conversion Date. Upon conversion of a Security, such Person shall
no longer be a Holder of such Security.

<P align="left" style="font-size: 10pt"><B>Dick&#146;s Sporting Goods Announces Successful Completion of Consent Solicitation</B>

<P align="left" style="font-size: 10pt">PITTSBURGH, Pa., December&nbsp;23, 2004 &#151; Dick&#146;s Sporting Goods, Inc. (NYSE: DKS)
announced today the successful completion of its previously announced solicitation to
amend the indenture related to its 2.375% Senior Convertible Notes due 2024 (CUSIP
Nos. 253393AA0 and 253393 AB 8) (the &#147;Notes&#148;).

<P align="left" style="font-size: 10pt">The amendment eliminates a provision in the indenture that prohibits the Company from
paying cash upon a conversion of the Notes if an event of default, as defined in the
indenture, has occurred and is continuing at that time.

<P align="left" style="font-size: 10pt">Georgeson Shareholder acted as information agent for the consent solicitation.
Merrill Lynch &#038; Co. acted as the solicitation agent.


<P align="left" style="font-size: 10pt"><B>About Dick&#146;s Sporting Goods, Inc.</B>

<P align="left" style="font-size: 10pt">Pittsburgh-based Dick&#146;s Sporting Goods, Inc. is an authentic full-line sporting goods
retailer offering a broad assortment of brand name sporting goods equipment, apparel,
and footwear in a specialty store environment. As of October&nbsp;30, 2004, the Company
operated 233 stores in 32 states primarily throughout the Eastern half of the U.S.
under the Dick&#146;s Sporting Goods and Galyan&#146;s names.

<P align="left" style="font-size: 10pt">Dick&#146;s Sporting Goods, Inc. news releases are available at
<U>http://www.dickssportinggoods.com/</U> (click on the Investor Relations link at the
bottom of the home page).
