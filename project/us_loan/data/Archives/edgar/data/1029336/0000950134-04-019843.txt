<P align="right" style="font-size: 10pt">Exhibit 99.1


<P align="center" style="font-size: 10pt">ACIH, INC. ANNOUNCES PRICING OF SENIOR DISCOUNT NOTES OFFERING


<P align="left" style="font-size: 10pt">DALLAS, TX (December&nbsp;22, 2004) &#150; ACIH, Inc. (&#147;ACIH&#148;), a Delaware corporation that will become the
parent company of Atrium Companies, Inc. (&#147;ACI&#148;), announced that on
December&nbsp;21, 2004 it priced an offering of $174.0&nbsp;million aggregate principal amount at maturity
($124.9&nbsp;million approximate gross proceeds) of 11&#189;% Senior Discount Notes due 2012. Interest will
accrue on the notes in the form of an increase in the accreted value of the notes prior to December
15, 2007. Thereafter, cash interest on the notes will accrue and be payable semiannually in
arrears, commencing in 2008, at the rate of 11&#189;% per annum. The notes will have an initial accreted
value of $717.91 per $1,000 principal amount at maturity of the notes.

<P align="left" style="font-size: 10pt">The notes were offered within the United States only to qualified institutional investors pursuant
to Rule&nbsp;144A under the Securities Act of 1933 (&#147;Securities Act&#148;) and outside the United States in
compliance with Regulation&nbsp;S of the Securities Act.

<P align="left" style="font-size: 10pt">The net proceeds of the offering will be used to consummate a comprehensive refinancing plan, which
includes, among other things, a cash tender offer and consent solicitation for all of ACI&#146;s
outstanding 10&#189;% Senior Subordinated Notes due 2009 and the repayment of all outstanding borrowings
under ACI&#146;s existing senior secured credit facility.

<P align="left" style="font-size: 10pt">The notes have not been registered under the Securities Act of 1933, as amended (the &#147;Securities
Act&#148;), or any state securities laws, and unless so registered, may not be offered or sold in the
United States, except pursuant to an exemption from, or in a transaction not subject to, the
registration requirements of the Securities Act and applicable state securities laws.

<P align="left" style="font-size: 10pt">This press release does not constitute an offer to sell or the solicitation of an offer to buy any
security and shall not constitute an offer, solicitation or sale in any jurisdiction in which such
offering would be unlawful.

<P align="left" style="font-size: 10pt">ACI, based in Dallas, Texas, is one of the largest manufacturers and suppliers of residential
windows in North America, with pro forma net sales exceeding $775&nbsp;million for the twelve months
ended September&nbsp;30, 2004, approximately 7,000 employees and 80 manufacturing facilities and
distribution centers in 22 states and Mexico.

<P align="left" style="font-size: 10pt">Statements in this press release, other than statements of historical information, are
forward-looking statements that are made pursuant to the safe harbor provisions of the Private
Securities Litigation Reform Act of 1995. Investors are cautioned that forward-looking statements
are inherently uncertain. Actual performance and results may differ materially from those projected
or suggested herein due to certain risks and uncertainties including, without limitation, operating
risks. Those and other risks are described in the ACI&#146;s filings with the Securities and Exchange
Commission (the &#147;SEC&#148;) made over the last 12&nbsp;months, copies of which are available from the SEC or
