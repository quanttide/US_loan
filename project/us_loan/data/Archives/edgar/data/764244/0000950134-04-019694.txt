<P align="center" style="font-size: 10pt"><B>Intervoice Announces Results for the Third Quarter of Fiscal 2005</B><BR>
<I>Sales of $48.4 Million and Net Income of $0.18 Per Diluted Share</I>


<P align="left" style="font-size: 10pt"><B>DALLAS &#151; December&nbsp;21, 2004 &#151; </B>Intervoice, Inc. (NASDAQ: INTV) today reported revenues of $48.4
million for its third fiscal quarter ended November&nbsp;30, 2004, a 16&nbsp;percent increase from $41.8
million posted in the same quarter last year. Net income for the third quarter of fiscal 2005 was
$7.0&nbsp;million, or $0.18 per diluted share. These results reflect significant improvement from net
income of $3.6&nbsp;million, or $0.10 per diluted share, posted in the third quarter of last year. Net
income for the third quarter of fiscal 2005 includes the positive effects of a tax settlement with
a foreign government totaling $0.9&nbsp;million, or $0.02 per diluted share, and several domestic tax
adjustments totaling $0.9&nbsp;million, or $0.02 per diluted share, resulting from the completion of
federal and state tax returns for fiscal 2004. Net income for the quarter was also affected by
$1.5&nbsp;million, or $0.04 per diluted share, in costs incurred in connection with the previously
disclosed Audit Committee investigation.

<P align="left" style="font-size: 10pt">Cash balances increased by $1.6&nbsp;million to $50.6&nbsp;million during the third quarter of fiscal 2005,
and the Company reduced its total debt balance to $10.4&nbsp;million through repayments totaling $1.1
million. Working capital increased from $29.7&nbsp;million at August&nbsp;31, 2004 to $40.6&nbsp;million at
November&nbsp;30, 2004.

<P align="left" style="font-size: 10pt">&#147;I am very pleased with
the significant growth we achieved in our solutions revenues,&#148; said Craig
Holmes, the Company&#146;s Executive Vice President and Chief Financial Officer. &#147;I am also encouraged
by the improved bottom line results and continued strengthening of our balance sheet. Overall, we
had outstanding financial results, which clearly exceeded our expectations.&#148;

<P align="left" style="font-size: 10pt">The Company&#146;s solutions backlog of $35.2&nbsp;million at November&nbsp;30, 2004, is up slightly from $34.0
million at November&nbsp;30, 2003 and down from $40.2&nbsp;million at the end of last quarter. The Company&#146;s
November&nbsp;30, 2004 backlog excludes two solutions orders totaling $3.8&nbsp;million related to contracts
signed by customers prior to quarter end but not received by the Company until after quarter end.
