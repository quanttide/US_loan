<p style="font-size:10.0pt;margin:0in 0in .0001pt;"><font face="Times New Roman">&nbsp;</font></p>

<p style="margin:0in 0in .0001pt;text-indent:.5in;"><font size="2" face="Times New Roman" style="font-size:10.0pt;">On December 20, 2004,
SI International, Inc. (&#147;SI International&#148;) closed the acquisition of Bridge
Technology Corporation, a provider of information technology and information
management, (&#147;Bridge Technology&#148;) pursuant to a Merger Agreement dated November
30, 2004 (the &#147;Agreement&#148;) by and among SI International, Bridge Technology,
and the Bridge Technology shareholders.&#160;
The terms of the Agreement provide that SI International acquired all of
the outstanding capital stock of Bridge Technology for $30 million in cash,
including funds available through SI International&#146;s credit facility.&#160; Pursuant to the terms and conditions of the
Agreement, a wholly-owned subsidiary of SI International to be formed for
purposes of the transaction will merge with and into Bridge Technology, with
Bridge Technology as the surviving corporation in the merger and continuing as
a wholly-owned subsidiary of SI International.&#160;
A portion of the purchase price will be held back by SI International
for a period of 18 months in order to secure the post-closing indemnity
obligations of the Bridge Technology shareholders.</font></p>

<p style="margin:0in 0in .0001pt;"><font size="2" face="Times New Roman">&nbsp;</font></p>

<p align="center" style="margin:0in 0in .0001pt;text-align:center;"><b><font size="2" face="Times New Roman" style="font-size:10.0pt;font-weight:bold;">Acquisition Broadens Customer Base with Intelligence Platform</font></b></p>

<p style="margin:0in 0in .0001pt;"><font size="2" face="Times New Roman">&nbsp;</font></p>

<p style="font-size:10.0pt;margin:0in 0in .0001pt;"><b><font size="2" face="Times New Roman" style="font-size:10.0pt;font-weight:bold;">RESTON, VIRGINIA &#150; December 21, 2004</font></b>
&#150; <i><font style="font-style:italic;">SI International, Inc. </font></i>(Nasdaq:SINT),
an information technology and network solutions (IT) company, announced today
that on December 20, 2004 it completed the purchase of Bridge Technology
Corporation, a provider of information technology and information management.&#160; The acquisition supports <i><font style="font-style:italic;">SI
International&#146;s</font></i> strategic growth plan to broaden its customer base
into the intelligence agencies, and strengthen its portfolio of
mission-critical solutions.</p>

<p style="margin:0in 0in .0001pt;"><font size="2" face="Times New Roman">&nbsp;</font></p>

<p style="margin:0in 0in .0001pt;"><font size="2" face="Times New Roman" style="font-size:10.0pt;">Established in 2000, Bridge Technology provides solutions to the
Defense intelligence agencies in areas such as program management, acquisition
management, logistics management, systems engineering, software engineering,
and business process reengineering.&#160; The
company has approximately 140 employees with approximately 90 percent holding
security clearances.&#160; Bridge Technology&#146;s
primary clients are major Defense intelligence agencies.</font></p>

<p style="margin:0in 0in .0001pt;"><font size="2" face="Times New Roman">&nbsp;</font></p>

<p style="margin:0in 0in .0001pt;"><font size="2" face="Times New Roman" style="font-size:10.0pt;">Under the terms of the agreement, <i><font style="font-style:italic;">SI
International</font></i> acquired Bridge Technology for $30 million in
cash.&#160; The transaction was funded through
cash-on-hand and borrowings under the company&#146;s credit facility.&#160; <i><font style="font-style:italic;">SI International</font></i> expects the acquisition to be
accretive to earnings.&#160; Bridge Technology&#146;s
trailing twelve months revenue ended September 30, 2004 was approximately $22.2
million and operating income of approximately $3.5 million.</font></p>

<p style="margin:0in 0in .0001pt;"><font size="2" face="Times New Roman">&nbsp;</font></p>

<p style="margin:0in 0in .0001pt;"><font size="2" face="Times New Roman" style="font-size:10.0pt;">&#147;The combination of Bridge Technology&#146;s
intelligence expertise with <i><font style="font-style:italic;">SI International&#146;s</font></i>
full array of IT and network solutions and resources enhances the portfolio of
capabilities that we provide, to more effectively serve the growing needs of
the Federal Government,&#148; said Ray Oleson, Chairman and CEO of <i><font style="font-style:italic;">SI International</font></i>.&#160; &#147;Bridge
Technology becomes the platform we use to address the intelligence market.&#148;</font></p>

<p style="margin:0in 0in .0001pt;"><font size="2" face="Times New Roman">&nbsp;</font></p>

<p style="margin:0in 0in .0001pt;"><font size="2" face="Times New Roman" style="font-size:10.0pt;">Bridge Technology will operate as a new
business unit within <i><font style="font-style:italic;">SI International</font></i>.&#160;&#160; Ron Dabbieri, co-founder of Bridge
Technology, will continue overseeing the business unit&#146;s day-to-day operations
and report directly to <i><font style="font-style:italic;">SI International</font></i>&#146;s
President and Chief Operating Officer, Brad Antle.</font></p>
