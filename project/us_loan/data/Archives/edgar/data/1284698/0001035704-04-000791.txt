<P align="left" style="font-size: 10pt">FOR IMMEDIATE RELEASE<BR>
December&nbsp;21, 2004


<P align="center" style="font-size: 12pt"><B>Liberty Media International Receives<BR>
Payment In Full On J-COM Loans</B>


<P align="left" style="font-size: 10pt">ENGLEWOOD, Colorado &#151; Liberty Media International, Inc. (LMI) (NASDAQ: LBTYA, LBTYB) today
announced that it has received &#165;42.8&nbsp;billion ($408&nbsp;million) in cash from its 45% owned affiliate,
Jupiter Telecommunications Co., Ltd. (J-COM) in full satisfaction of all shareholder loans,
including accrued interest thereon, owed by J-COM to LMI.

<P align="left" style="font-size: 10pt">As a result of the refinancing of certain of its indebtedness, J-COM was able to repay all of its
shareholder loans and eliminate a significant portion of the shareholder guarantees. At September
30, 2004, the total principal amount of J-COM&#146;s shareholder loans was &#165;124.6&nbsp;billion ($1.1
billion), of which &#165;41.3&nbsp;billion ($375&nbsp;million) was owed to LMI.

<P align="left" style="font-size: 10pt">Liberty Media International, Inc. (NASDAQ: LBTYA, LBTYB) owns interests in broadband distribution
and content companies operating outside the U.S., principally in Europe, Asia, and Latin America.
Through its subsidiaries and affiliates, LMI is one of the leading broadband services providers
outside the United States. LMI&#146;s businesses include UnitedGlobalCom, Inc., Jupiter
Telecommunications Co., Ltd., Jupiter Programming Co., Ltd., Liberty Cablevision of Puerto Rico
Ltd. and Pramer S.C.A.
