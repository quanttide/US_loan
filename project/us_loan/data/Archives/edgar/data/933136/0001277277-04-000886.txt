<p align="center"><b><font size="2" face="Times New Roman">SECTION 1 - REGISTRANT'S BUSINESS AND OPERATIONS</font></b></p>

<p><b><font size="2" face="Times New Roman">Item 1.01 Entry into a Material
Definitive Agreement.</font></b></p>

<p class="MsoNormal" style='text-indent:.5in'><font size="2" face="Times New Roman">On December 20, 2004, Washington Mutual, Inc.
(the &ldquo;<u>Company</u>&rdquo;) entered into an Employment Agreement with Stephen J. Rotella, pursuant to which the Company will
employ Mr. Rotella as its President and Chief Operating Officer (the &ldquo;<u>Employment Agreement</u>&rdquo;).&nbsp; The
Employment Agreement is dated effective as of January 10, 2005, and contains the terms described below.&nbsp; The following summary
description is qualified in its entirety by reference to the Employment Agreement, which is attached to this Form 8-K as Exhibit
10.1, and is incorporated herein by reference.</font></p>

<p class="MsoNormal" style='margin-left:.75in;text-indent:-.5in'><font size="2" face="Times New Roman">-&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;</font><font face="Times New Roman"><font size="2">
</font>
<u><font size="2">Duties</font></u><font size="2">.&nbsp; Mr. Rotella will have the title of President and Chief Operating Officer
and shall at all times retain either title, unless he agrees otherwise and any such change does not diminish, reduce or impair his
authority, responsibilities or duties.&nbsp; Mr. Rotella&rsquo;s duties shall be reasonably directed by the Company&rsquo;s Board
of Directors from time to time, and shall include, in cooperation with the Company&rsquo;s Chief Executive Officer, responsibility
for the operating strategies of the Company and other customary duties.&nbsp; Mr. Rotella will report to Kerry K. Killinger, the
Company&rsquo;s Chairman and Chief Executive Officer.</font></font></p>

<p class="MsoNormal" style='margin-left:.75in;text-indent:-.5in'><font size="2" face="Times New Roman">-&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;</font><font face="Times New Roman"><font size="2">
</font>
<u><font size="2">Compensation</font></u><font size="2">.&nbsp; Mr. Rotella&rsquo;s compensation shall be determined by the Human
Resources Committee of the Company&rsquo;s Board of Directors (the &ldquo;<u>HR Committee</u>&rdquo;) and will be reviewed
annually.&nbsp; During his employment, however, there may be no reduction in Mr. Rotella&rsquo;s base salary, target bonus, stock
options, restricted stock and performance share grant amounts without his consent and the Company has no implied obligation to
raise his compensation at any time.&nbsp; Mr. Rotella&rsquo;s starting annual salary will be $900,000 and his 2005 target cash
performance bonus will be $2,500,000.&nbsp; The bonus will be paid pursuant to the Company&rsquo;s Leadership Bonus Plan, and for
2005 is guaranteed to be not less than $2,000,000.&nbsp; In addition, the Company will pay Mr. Rotella a one-time cash payment of
$2,600,000 upon his commencement of employment.&nbsp; Mr. Rotella will also be enrolled as a participant in all employee benefit
plans (including retirement and insurance plans) available to other Company officers, and will be entitled to receive such other
perquisites as the Board or the HR Committee may from time to time deem appropriate.</font></font></p>

<p class="MsoNormal" style='margin-left:.75in;text-indent:-.5in'><font size="2" face="Times New Roman">-&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;</font><font face="Times New Roman"><font size="2">
</font>
<u><font size="2">Restricted Stock</font></u><font size="2">.&nbsp; On his first day of employment, Mr. Rotella will receive a
one-time award of 220,000 restricted shares of Company common stock pursuant to the Company&rsquo;s Equity Incentive Plan.&nbsp;
The sale and transfer restrictions on the shares will lapse pursuant to the following vesting schedule:&nbsp; 40% of the shares
vest on July 31, 2005, 35% of the shares vest on January 31, 2006 and 25% of the shares vest on January 31, 2010.</font></font></p>

<p class="MsoNormal" style='margin-left:.75in;text-indent:-.5in'><font size="2" face="Times New Roman">-&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;</font><font face="Times New Roman"><font size="2">
</font>
<u><font size="2">Employment Term</font></u><font size="2">.&nbsp; Mr. Rotella&rsquo;s employment may be terminated by the
Company&rsquo;s Board of Directors in its sole discretion, or Mr. Rotella in his sole discretion, at any time.&nbsp; In the event
he is terminated or resigns upon or within three years after a &ldquo;change in control&rdquo; (as defined in Section 11 of the
Employment Agreement) of the Company, is terminated by the Company other than for &ldquo;cause&rdquo; (as defined in Section 6(i)
of the Employment Agreement), or dies or becomes disabled while employed by the Company, (i) all stock options held by Mr. Rotella
shall become immediately vested and exercisable until the earlier of the second anniversary of his termination date or the
options&rsquo; expiration date; (ii) the restrictions on Mr. Rotella&rsquo;s restricted stock will immediately lapse (subject to
exclusion by the HR Committee of any grants made after March 1, 2005); and (iii) all performance share awards shall vest and become
payable to Mr. Rotella in accordance with their terms after completion of the applicable performance period.&nbsp; In addition, Mr.
Rotellas is entitled to the following benefits:</font></font></p>

<p><b><font face="Times New Roman" size="2"></font></b></p>

<hr>

<p class="MsoNormal" style='margin-left:1.0in;text-indent:-.5in'><font size="2" face="Times New Roman">-&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;</font><font face="Times New Roman"><font size="2">
</font>
<u><font size="2">Termination After a Change in Control</font></u><font size="2">.&nbsp; If Mr. Rotella is terminated for any
reason or resigns for &ldquo;good cause&rdquo; (as defined in Section 12 of the Employment Agreement) upon or within three years
after a &ldquo;change in control&rdquo; (as defined in Section 11 of the Employment Agreement) of the Company, the Company or its
successor will pay Mr. Rotella an amount equal to three times his &ldquo;annual compensation&rdquo; (as defined in Section 6(d) of
the Employment Agreement).&nbsp; If any of these benefits payable to Mr. Rotella are deemed to constitute a &ldquo;parachute
payment&rdquo; under Section 280G of the Internal Revenue Code of 1986, as amended, the Company will pay Mr. Rotella certain tax
gross-up payments as described in Section 6(e) of the Employment Agreement.</font></font></p>

<p class="MsoNormal" style='margin-left:1.0in;text-indent:-.5in'><font size="2" face="Times New Roman">-&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;</font><font face="Times New Roman"><font size="2">
</font>
<u><font size="2">Termination by the Company without Cause</font></u><font size="2">.&nbsp; If Mr. Rotella is terminated by the
Company other than for &ldquo;cause&rdquo; (as defined in Section 6(i) of the Employment Agreement), then upon executing the
Company&rsquo;s form Separation Agreement, Mr. Rotella will be entitled to the benefits described
in (i), (ii) and (iii) above and an amount equal to two
times his &ldquo;annual compensation&rdquo; (as defined in Section 6(d) of the Employment Agreement).</font></font></p>

<p class="MsoNormal" style='margin-left:1.0in;text-indent:-.5in'><font size="2" face="Times New Roman">-&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;</font><font face="Times New Roman"><font size="2">
</font>
<u><font size="2">Termination Upon Death or Disability</font></u><font size="2">.&nbsp; Upon Mr. Rotella&rsquo;s death or
disability, his employment shall terminate, and neither he nor his estate shall be entitled to further compensation from the
Company pursuant to the Employment Agreement, except for payments under life and disability insurance policies, any sums which may
be available under applicable employee benefit plans, and the amounts described above.&nbsp;</font></font></p>

<p class="MsoNormal" style='text-align:justify'><font size="2" face="Times New Roman">&nbsp;</font></p>

<p align="center"><font size="2" face="Times New Roman">
<b>SECTION 5 &ndash; CORPORATE GOVERNANCE AND MANAGEMENT</b></font></p>

<p class="MsoNormal" style='text-align:justify'><b><font size="2" face="Times New Roman">Item 5.02 &nbsp;Departure of Directors or Principal Officers; Election of Directors;
Appointment of Principal Officers.</font></b></p>

<p class="MsoNormal" style='text-indent:.5in'><font size="2" face="Times New Roman">On December 21, 2004, the Company announced the
appointment of Stephen J. Rotella as the Company&rsquo;s new President and Chief Operating Officer.&nbsp; Mr. Rotella will assume
his new position with the Company on January 10, 2005 and will report to Kerry K. Killinger, the Company&rsquo;s Chairman and Chief
Executive Officer.&nbsp; The Company will employ Mr. Rotella pursuant to the terms of the Employment Agreement attached to this
Form 8-K as Exhibit 10.1, and as described in this Form 8-K above under Item 1.01.&nbsp; Mr. Rotella, age 51, served on the JP
Morgan Chase Executive Committee and has been the Chief Executive Officer, Chase Home Finance and EVP, JP Morgan Chase since
2001.&nbsp; At Chase, he led all residential lending and had responsibility for the capital markets, portfolio management,
marketing, credit, human resources, finance and legal activities of those businesses.&nbsp; He was also responsible for JP Morgan
Chase&rsquo;s Community Development Group.&nbsp; Previously, Mr. Rotella was the
Chief Operating Officer, Chase Home Finance from 1998 to 2001.</font></p>

<p class="MsoNormal" style='text-indent:.5in'><font size="2" face="Times New Roman">&nbsp;</font></p>

<p class="MsoNormal" style='text-indent:.5in'><font size="2" face="Times New Roman">Kerry Killinger held the position of President
of the Company prior to the appointment of Mr. Rotella.&nbsp; Mr. Killinger will remain the Chairman and Chief Executive Officer of
the Company.</font></p>

<p class="MsoNormal" align="center" style='text-align:center;text-autospace:none'><b><font size="2" face="Arial">EMPLOYMENT
AGREEMENT</font></b></p>

<p class="MsoNormal" style='text-autospace:none'><font size="2" face="Arial">&nbsp;</font></p>

<p class="MsoNormal" style='text-autospace:none'><font size="2" face="Arial">This Employment Agreement (the "Agreement") is between
WASHINGTON MUTUAL, INC., a Washington corporation ("Washington Mutual") and Stephen J. Rotella ("Employee").</font></p>

<p class="MsoNormal" style='text-autospace:none'><font size="2" face="Arial">Employee has many years of experience in the financial
services business. &nbsp;Because of Employee's importance to Washington Mutual and the value to be derived from Employee's
continued employment, it is the desire of Washington Mutual and Employee to set forth certain terms and conditions relating to
Employee's employment as an inducement for Employee continuing his employment for so long as Washington Mutual desires to employ
Employee.</font></p>

<p class="MsoNormal" style='text-autospace:none'><font size="2" face="Arial">Therefore, the parties agree as follows:</font></p>

<p class="MsoNormal" style='text-autospace:none'><font size="2" face="Arial">1.&nbsp; <u>Employment - Term</u> &ndash; Washington
Mutual agrees to, and does hereby, employ Employee, and Employee agrees to, and does hereby, accept such employment, subject the
terms in this agreement and to an offer letter, dated December 17, 2004, which is incorporated herein by reference.
&nbsp;Employee's employment and this Agreement both shall remain in effect until Employee's employment and this Agreement are
simultaneously terminated in accordance with the terms hereof.</font></p>

<p class="MsoNormal" style='text-autospace:none'><font size="2" face="Arial">2.&nbsp; <u>Duties</u> &ndash; Employee shall perform
such duties as the Board of Directors of Washington Mutual (the "Board") may from time to time reasonably direct consistent with
this paragraph. &nbsp;(As used herein "Board" shall include the board of directors or other successor body performing their
function in the event of a Change in Control as defined below.) &nbsp;Employee shall have the title of President and Chief
Operating Officer. Employee's titles may be changed from time to time as the Board may determine, provided that Employee shall at
all times retain the title of either President or Chief Operating Officer unless he agrees otherwise and any such change shall not
diminish, reduce, or impair Employee&rsquo;s authority, responsibilities, or duties. &nbsp;Employee shall report to the Chairman
and Chief Executive Officer.&nbsp; Employee's duties shall include responsibility, in cooperation with the Chief Executive Officer,
for the operating strategies of Washington Mutual and other duties customarily performed by a person in the role of president and
chief operating officer in a corporation of the size and nature of Washington Mutual.</font></p>

<p class="MsoNormal" style='text-autospace:none'><font size="2" face="Arial">3.&nbsp; <u>Compensation</u> &ndash; During Employee's
employment under this Agreement, Employee shall receive base salary compensation in the amount determined by the Human Resources
Committee of the Board of Directors (the "HR Committee"), payable semi-monthly or in such manner as is consistent with Washington
Mutual's policy relating to salaried employees. &nbsp;In addition, Employee is entitled to participate in Washington Mutual's
Leadership Bonus Plan or such successor plan as may be adopted by the HR Committee, under which Employee may receive, subject to
the terms of the Plan, a bonus based on Washington Mutual's achievement of specified financial goals. &nbsp;Employee may also be
awarded stock options, restricted stock, and/or performance shares as determined by the HR Committee. &nbsp;Employee's compensation
shall be reviewed by the HR Committee annually and, if in their discretion it appears appropriate, such compensation shall be
adjusted provided that: (a) there may be no reduction in base salary, level of bonus, stock options, restricted stock, and
performance shares available to Employee without Employee's consent and (b) Washington Mutual has no implied obligation to raise
Employee's compensation.</font></p>

<p class="MsoNormal" style='text-autospace:none'>&nbsp;</p>

<p class="MsoNormal" style='text-autospace:none'><font size="2" face="Arial">4.&nbsp; <u>Other Benefits</u> &ndash; Subject to the
respective eligibility requirements and other terms and provisions of the applicable benefit or insurance plans (including relevant
waiting periods), Employee shall be enrolled as a participant in all employee benefit plans (including retirement and insurance
plans) available to other officers of Washington Mutual, as the same may from time to time be adopted or amended. &nbsp;Employee
shall also be entitled to receive such other perquisites as the Board or the HR Committee may from time to time deem
appropriate.</font></p>

<p class="MsoNormal" style='text-autospace:none'><font size="2" face="Arial">5.&nbsp; <u>Performance of Duties</u> &ndash;
&nbsp;Employee agrees that during his employment with Washington Mutual: (a) Employee will faithfully perform the duties of such
office or offices as he may occupy, which duties shall be such as may be assigned to him by the Board; (b) Employee will devote to
the performance of his duties all such time and attention as the Board shall reasonably require, taking, however, from time to time
such reasonable vacations as are consistent with his duties and Washington Mutual policy; and (c) Employee will not, without the
express consent of the Board, become actively associated with or engaged in any business or activity during the term of this
Agreement other than that of Washington Mutual (excepting of course customary family and personal activities which may include
management of personal investments so long as it does not entail active involvement in a business enterprise) and Employee will do
nothing inconsistent with his duties to Washington Mutual. &nbsp;Subject to the provisions of Washington Mutual&rsquo;s policy and
the approval of its Chief Executive Officer and General Counsel, Employee shall be allowed to serve as a member of one other board
of directors provided that such service does not unreasonably interfere with his duties hereunder.</font></p>

<p class="MsoNormal" style='text-autospace:none'><font size="2" face="Arial">6.&nbsp; <u>Termination</u> &ndash; (a) The Board may
terminate Employee's employment at any time in its sole discretion, and Employee may terminate Employee's employment in his sole
discretion. &nbsp;Except as expressly provided in this Agreement, upon termination of employment Washington Mutual shall have no
liability to pay any further compensation or any other benefit or sum whatsoever to Employee.</font></p>

<p class="MsoNormal" style='text-indent:.25in;text-autospace:none'><font size="2" face="Arial">(b)&nbsp; Upon termination of
employment, Employee's rights under all employee pension plans, employee welfare benefit plans, bonus plans and stock option and
restricted stock plans shall be determined under the terms of the plans and grants themselves except as otherwise specifically
provided in this Agreement.</font></p>

<p class="MsoNormal" style='text-indent:.25in;text-autospace:none'><font size="2" face="Arial">(c)&nbsp; If Employee's employment
is terminated by Washington Mutual for any reason upon or within three years after a Change in Control (as defined below) or
Employee resigns for "good cause" (as defined below) upon or within three years after a Change in Control, Employee shall be
entitled to receive, within five business days after the effective date of such termination or resignation, from Washington Mutual
or its successor, an amount equal to three times Employee's annual compensation. &nbsp;In addition, upon such an event:</font></p>

<p class="MsoNormal" style='margin-left:.25in;text-autospace:none'><font size="2" face="Arial">(1) &nbsp;all stock options held by
Employee shall become immediately vested and exercisable and shall continue to be exercisable until the earlier of (i) the two-year
anniversary of the effective date of Employee&rsquo;s termination or resignation or (ii) the expiration of the respective terms of
the options (unless earlier paid out under Section 15.3.1 of the Washington Mutual Inc. 2003 Equity Incentive Plan),
notwithstanding any provisions in the grant of such options regarding vesting,</font></p>

<p class="MsoNormal" style='margin-left:.25in;text-autospace:none'><font size="2" face="Arial">(2)&nbsp; the lapse of the
restrictions on Employee's restricted stock shall automatically be accelerated; provided that the HR Committee may exclude any
particular grant(s) of restricted stock made after March 1, 2005, from the acceleration provided for in this subsection (2),
and</font></p>

<p class="MsoNormal" style='margin-left:.25in;text-autospace:none'><font size="2" face="Arial">(3)&nbsp; all performance share
awards shall vest and shall be payable to Employee following the completion of their respective performance periods in accordance
with their terms, unless such awards shall be payable earlier under the terms of the grant or related plan.</font></p>

<p class="MsoNormal" style='text-indent:.25in;text-autospace:none'><font size="2" face="Arial">(d)&nbsp; For purposes of Section
6(c), Employee's "annual compensation" shall include all items of compensation provided by Washington Mutual other than the value
of stock options and/or restricted stock granted to Employee. &nbsp;Employee's "annual compensation" shall include the greater of
(i) the total of Employee's salary and target bonus for the calendar year in which the termination occurs (if established before
the termination) or (ii) Employee's salary and actual bonus for the prior calendar year (annualized if Employee was not employed by
Washington Mutual for the entire previous calendar year). Employee's "annual compensation" shall also include the amount of the
contributions made or anticipated to have been made on Employee's behalf to Washington Mutual's benefit plans for the calendar year
in which the termination occurs, including without limitation contributions to pension plans and plans qualified under Section 125
of the Internal Revenue Code of 1986 (cafeteria plans).</font></p>

<p class="MsoNormal" style='text-indent:.25in;text-autospace:none'><font size="2" face="Arial">(e)&nbsp; If Employee becomes
entitled to the payments and equity acceleration described in Section 6(c) (collectively the "Severance Payments"), and if any of
the Severance Payments constitute a "parachute payment" under Section 280G of the Internal Revenue Code of 1986 (the "Code"), as
amended, or any successor statute then in effect, then Washington Mutual shall pay an additional amount (the "Gross-Up Payment") to
Employee at the time specified in the following paragraph. &nbsp;The Gross-Up Payment shall be equal to the amount necessary so
that the net amount retained by Employee, after subtracting the parachute excise tax imposed by Section 4999 of the Code, as
amended, or any successor statute then in effect (the "Excise Tax"), and after also subtracting all federal, state or local income
tax, FICA tax and Excise Tax on the Gross-Up Payment, shall be equal to the net amount Employee would have retained if no Excise
Tax had been imposed and no Gross-Up Payment had been paid. &nbsp;The amount of the Gross-Up Payment shall be determined in good
faith by independent accountants or tax counsel selected by Washington Mutual and acceptable to Employee, who shall apply the
following assumptions: (i) Employee shall be treated as paying federal income taxes at the highest marginal rate in the calendar
year in which the Gross-Up Payment is made, and (ii) Employee shall be treated as paying state and local income taxes at the
highest marginal rate(s) in the calendar year in which the Gross-Up Payment is made in the locality of Employee's residence as of
the effective date of Employee's termination or resignation, net of the maximum reduction in federal income taxes that could be
obtained from deducting those state and local taxes.</font></p>

<p class="MsoNormal" style='text-indent:.25in;text-autospace:none'><font size="2" face="Arial">(f)&nbsp; The Gross-Up Payment shall
be made within five business days after the effective date of Employee's termination or resignation, provided that if the Gross-Up
Payment cannot be determined within that time, Washington Mutual shall pay Employee within that time an estimate, determined in
good faith by Washington Mutual, of the minimum amount of the Gross-Up Payment and shall pay the remainder (plus interest at the
rate provided in Section 1274(d)(1)(A) of the Code) as soon as the amount can be determined but in no event later than the 30th day
after the effective date of Employee's termination or resignation. If the estimated payment is more than the amount later
determined to have been due, the excess (plus interest at the rate provided in Section 1274(d)(1)(A) of the Code) shall be repaid
by Employee within five business days after written demand.</font></p>

imposed is less than the amount that was taken into account in determining the amount of the Gross-Up Payment, Employee shall repay
at the time that the amount of the reduced Excise Tax is finally determined the portion of the Gross-Up Payment attributable to
that reduction (plus the portion of the Gross-Up Payment attributable to the Excise Tax, FICA tax and federal, state and local
income tax imposed on the portion of the Gross-Up Payment being repaid by Employee, to the extent the repayment results in a
reduction in or refund of Excise Tax, FICA tax or federal, state or local income tax), plus interest on the amount of the repayment
at the rate provided in Section 1274(d)(1)(A) of the Code. If the actual Excise Tax imposed is more than the amount that was taken
into account in determining the amount of the Gross-Up Payment, Washington Mutual shall make an additional gross-up payment in
respect of such excess (plus interest at the rate provided in Section 1274(d)(1)(A) of the Code) at the time that the amount of the
excess is finally determined.</font></p>

<p class="MsoNormal" style='text-indent:.25in;text-autospace:none'><font size="2" face="Arial">(h)&nbsp; If Employee&rsquo;s
employment is terminated by Washington Mutual at any time other than upon or within three years after a Change in Control for any
reason other than for cause (as defined below) and Employee executes a Separation Agreement in the form attached hereto as Exhibit
B, Employee shall be entitled to receive, from Washington Mutual or its successor, within five business days after Employee
executes and returns the Separation Agreement, an amount equal to two times Employee's annual compensation, as defined in Section
6(d). &nbsp;In addition, upon such an event</font></p>

<p class="MsoNormal" style='margin-left:.25in;text-autospace:none'><font size="2" face="Arial">(1)&nbsp; all stock options held by

anniversary of the effective date of Employee&rsquo;s termination or resignation or (ii) the expiration of the respective terms of

notwithstanding any provisions in the grant of such options regarding vesting,</font></p>

<p class="MsoNormal" style='margin-left:.25in;text-autospace:none'><font size="2" face="Arial">(2)&nbsp; the lapse of the

particular grant(s) of restricted stock made after March 1, 2005 from the acceleration provided for in this subsection (2),
and</font></p>

<p class="MsoNormal" style='margin-left:.25in;text-autospace:none'><font size="2" face="Arial">(3)&nbsp; all performance share
awards shall vest and shall be payable to Employee following the completion of their respective performance periods in accordance
with their terms, unless such awards shall be payable earlier under the terms of the grant or related plan.</font></p>

<p class="MsoNormal" style='text-indent:.25in;text-autospace:none'><font size="2" face="Arial">(i)&nbsp; Termination of Employee's
employment hereunder for "cause" shall mean termination because (i) Employee engages in abusive use of alcohol or other drugs on a
continuing or recurring basis, (ii) Employee is convicted of any felony or of a misdemeanor involving moral turpitude (including
forgery, fraud, theft, or embezzlement), or is convicted or enters into a pretrial diversion or similar program in connection with
the criminal prosecution for an offense involving dishonesty, breach of trust or money laundering, or (iii) Employee engages in
dishonesty, fraud, willful destruction or theft of property of Washington Mutual or a Subsidiary, physical attack on another
employee, or engages in (x) willful malfeasance, willful gross neglect in the performance of his duties, or (z) willful misconduct
which, in any circumstance described in (x), (y), or (z), is materially injurious to Washington Mutual or a Subsidiary. &nbsp;For
purposes of this Agreement, no act, or failure to act, shall be deemed &ldquo;willful&rdquo; unless done by Employee without a good
faith belief that it was in, or not contrary to, the best interests of Washington Mutual or any Subsidiary.&nbsp; In addition, on
and after Employee&rsquo;s 65<sup>th</sup> birthday, and provided the termination is not done upon or within three years after a
Change in Control, "cause" shall include a reasonable, good-faith determination by the Board that Employee has failed to properly
perform or fulfill the duties of his office. &nbsp;Notwithstanding anything to the contrary set forth herein, Employee shall not be
terminated for &ldquo;cause&rdquo; under clause (iii) of this paragraph unless after receiving written notice stating the basis for
termination, Employee is given fifteen (15) days to cure the neglect or conduct.</font></p>

We currently offer both a 401(k) plan (&ldquo;WaMu Savings&rdquo;) and a cash balance pension plan (&ldquo;WaMu Pension&rdquo;).
WaMu Savings allows you to save for retirement by contributing a percentage of your salary to the plan on a pretax basis. You are
eligible to join WaMu Savings on the first day of the month following your date of hire. Washington Mutual matches your pretax
contributions to WaMu Savings effective the first day of the month after you have completed twelve months of service. The matching
contribution is currently 100% on the first 3% of your pretax contributions of your eligible compensation, and 50% on the next 2%
of your pretax contributions of your eligible compensation. You will be immediately vested in any matching contributions. You are
eligible for benefit accruals under the WaMu Pension on the first day of the quarter following your one year anniversary.
Washington Mutual reserves the right to amend or terminate these plans at any time.</font></p>

<p class="MsoNormal" align="right" style='text-align:right'><b>
<font size="2" face="Times New Roman">EXHIBIT 99.1</font></b></p>

<p class="MsoNormal" align="right" style='text-align:right'>
<font size="2" face="Times New Roman">&nbsp;December 21, 2004<br>
</font><b><u><font size="2" face="Times New Roman">FOR IMMEDIATE RELEASE</font></u></b></p>

<p class="MsoNormal"><font size="2" face="Times New Roman">&nbsp;</font></p>

<p class="MsoNormal"><font size="2" face="Times New Roman">&nbsp;</font></p>

<p class="MsoNormal" align="center" style='text-align:center'><b>
<font size="2" face="Times New (W1)">Washington Mutual Names Stephen J.
Rotella<br>
President and Chief Operating Officer&nbsp;</font></b></p>

<p class="MsoNormal" align="center" style='text-align:center'><b>
<font size="2" face="Times New (W1)">Move is Next Step in Company&rsquo;s Growth and
Evolution</font></b></p>

<p class="MsoNormal" style='text-indent:.5in;line-height:200%'>
<font size="2" face="Times New Roman">SEATTLE, WA &mdash; Washington Mutual, Inc. (NYSE: WM) Chairman and Chief Executive Officer
Kerry Killinger announced today that JP Morgan Chase executive <i>&nbsp;</i> Steve Rotella,
51, has been named President and Chief Operating Officer (COO).&nbsp;</font></p>

<p class="MsoNormal" style='text-indent:.5in;line-height:200%'>
<font size="2" face="Times New Roman">&nbsp;"We embark on our new five-year strategic plan as a far larger and more complex company
than we were just five years ago, and so the addition of a President and COO to our team is the natural next step in our growth and
evolution as a&nbsp; top-tier, national financial services company,"&nbsp;Killinger said. &ldquo;Steve&rsquo;s demonstrated track
record as a terrific executive with strong operational skills will help greatly in the execution of our strategic
vision.&rdquo;</font></p>

<p class="MsoNormal" style='text-indent:.5in;line-height:200%'>
<font size="2" face="Times New Roman">Rotella, who assumes his new position on January 10, 2005, will be based at the company&rsquo;s
headquarters in Seattle, Wash. He will be responsible for oversight of the retail, commercial and mortgage lines of business as
well as the company&rsquo;s technology group and day-to-day administration. Rotella will report directly to
Killinger.</font></p>

<p class="MsoNormal" style='text-indent:.5in;line-height:200%'>
<font size="2" face="Times New Roman">The company noted that the appointment is the latest step in its continuing commitment to add
new management talent where appropriate in order to achieve operational excellence company-wide.</font></p>

<p class="MsoNormal" style='text-indent:.5in;line-height:200%'>
<font size="2" face="Times New Roman">"I have never been more excited about the opportunities to strengthen and grow our company in
the years ahead,&rdquo; Killinger said. &ldquo;Steve is a wonderful addition to our strong core executive team which continues to
make great progress toward our goal of becoming the premier retailer of financial services for consumers and small businesses. In
Steve, we&rsquo;ve found an executive with the skill and experience to accelerate our progress,&nbsp;and who also shares our
values.&rdquo;</font></p>

<hr>

<p class="MsoNormal" style='text-indent:.5in;line-height:200%'>&nbsp;</p>

<p class="MsoNormal" style='text-indent:.5in;line-height:200%'>
<font size="2" face="Times New Roman">Rotella was a member of the JP Morgan Chase Executive Committee and responsible for the Chase
Home Finance business, where he managed a team of approximately 18,000 in nearly 300 locations across the
country.&nbsp;</font></p>

<p class="MsoNormal" style='text-indent:.5in;line-height:200%'>
<font size="2" face="Times New Roman">&ldquo;I have admired Washington Mutual&rsquo;s quick ascent from a small regional player to a
national leader in the financial services industry. I know that Washington Mutual&rsquo;s customer-centric style of banking, deep
commitment to the communities it serves and employee-driven culture are a perfect fit for me&mdash;and a reflection of
Kerry&rsquo;s strategic approach to redefining financial services.&rdquo;</font></p>

<p class="MsoNormal" style='text-indent:.5in;line-height:200%'>
<font size="2" face="Times New Roman">Rotella added: &ldquo;I&rsquo;m looking forward to meeting Washington Mutual&rsquo;s employees
and helping lead our team as we work to execute Kerry&rsquo;s vision.&rdquo;&nbsp;</font></p>

<p class="MsoNormal" style='text-indent:.25in;line-height:200%'>
<font size="2" face="Times New Roman">The company said that it remains focused on executing its core strategies<b>;</b> including:</font></p>

<p class="MsoNormal" style='margin-left:.75in;text-indent:-.25in;line-height: 200%'><font size="2" face="Symbol">&middot;<font face="Times New Roman">&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;</font></font><font size="2"> Continuing to grow its superior
retail banking model with profitable customer growth, and opening approximately 250 new retail banking stores each year for the
foreseeable future;</font></p>

<p class="MsoNormal" style='margin-left:.75in;text-indent:-.25in;line-height: 200%'><font size="2" face="Symbol">&middot;<font face="Times New Roman">&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;</font></font><font size="2"> Transforming the company&rsquo;s
mortgage business and maintaining a leading national position in mortgage lending while focusing on profitable market share;</font></p>

<p class="MsoNormal" style='margin-left:.75in;text-indent:-.25in;line-height: 200%'><font size="2" face="Symbol">&middot;<font face="Times New Roman">&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;</font></font><font size="2"> Continuing to grow its commercial
business, including its market-leading multi-family lending operation and;</font></p>

<p class="MsoNormal" style='margin-left:.75in;text-indent:-.25in;line-height: 200%'><font size="2" face="Symbol">&middot;<font face="Times New Roman">&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;</font></font><font size="2"> Maintaining risk management as a
top priority, including enhancing the company&rsquo;s MSR management;</font></p>

<p class="MsoNormal" style='text-indent:.25in;line-height:200%'>
<font size="2" face="Times New Roman">"I've been particularly pleased with the success of the retail bank as well as the progress
we're making in our mortgage operations and our cost leadership initiatives in recent months,&rdquo; Killinger said. &ldquo;Our
teams are focused on delivering great service to our customers and we enter 2005 with good momentum on all these fronts. Now it's
time to ensure that all of our operations are aligned, coordinated, and operating with industry-leading efficiency. The addition of
Steve will help us do exactly that."&nbsp;</font></p>

<hr>

<p class="MsoNormal" style='text-indent:.25in;line-height:200%'>&nbsp;</p>

<p class="MsoNormal" style='text-indent:.5in;line-height:200%'>
<font size="2" face="Times New Roman">Rotella, who serves on the JP Morgan Chase Executive Committee, has been the chief executive
officer, Chase Home Finance, and EVP, JP Morgan Chase since 2001. There, he led all residential lending, directly managing the
prime, sub-prime and home equity businesses. He also had responsibility for the capital markets, portfolio management, marketing,
credit, human resources, finance and legal activities of those businesses.&nbsp; He was also responsible for JP Morgan
Chase&rsquo;s Community Development Group.&nbsp; Previously, he was the chief operating officer, Chase Home Finance from 1998 to
2001.</font></p>

<p class="MsoNormal" style='text-indent:.25in;line-height:200%'>
<font size="2" face="Times New Roman">From 1991 through 1998 he served as EVP, Servicing at Chase Manhattan Mortgage Company.&nbsp;
From 1987 through 1991, Rotella held various positions with Chase&rsquo;s mortgage business including Senior Vice President,
Marketing/Finance/Product Development. &nbsp;Prior to joining Chase, Rotella worked in the retail brokerage, mutual fund, and
systems consulting industries.</font></p>

<p class="MsoNormal" style='text-indent:.25in;line-height:200%'>
<font size="2" face="Times New Roman">His strong belief in giving back to his community and his industry is reflected in his service
as chair of Chase&rsquo;s Housing Advisory Council, which focuses on increasing lending to underserved communities; a member of the
Board of Directors of the Mortgage Bankers Association; serving on the Executive Committee of the Financial Services
Roundtable&rsquo;s Housing Policy Council; a member and past president of the Consumer Mortgage Coalition; and former Chair of a
large regional arts company in Columbus, Ohio called BalletMet.</font></p>

<p class="MsoNormal" style='text-indent:.5in;line-height:200%'>
<font size="2" face="Times New Roman">A native of New York, Rotella attended State University of New York in Stony Brook, and
received his Bachelor&rsquo;s degree in Economics in 1975.&nbsp; He earned his MBA in Finance and Information Systems in 1978 from
State University of New York in Albany.&nbsp;</font></p>

<hr>

<p class="MsoNormal"><b><font size="2" face="Times New Roman">&nbsp;</font></b></p>

<p class="MsoNormal"><b><font size="2" face="Times New Roman">About Washington
Mutual</font></b></p>

<p class="MsoNormal" style='text-indent:.5in;line-height:200%'>
<font size="2" face="Times New Roman">With a history dating back to 1889, Washington Mutual is a retailer of financial services that
provides a diversified line of products and services to consumers and commercial clients. At September 30, 2004, Washington Mutual
and its subsidiaries had assets of $288.83 billion. Washington Mutual currently operates more than 2,300 retail banking, mortgage
lending, commercial banking and financial services offices throughout the nation. Washington Mutual&rsquo;s press releases are
available at www.wamunewsroom.com.</font></p>
