<P align="left" style="font-size: 10pt"><B>Item&nbsp;2.01. Completion of Acquisition or Disposition of Assets.</B>


<P align="left" style="font-size: 10pt">On October&nbsp;28, 2004, First Potomac Realty Trust (the &#147;Company&#148;) filed a current report on Form 8-K
announcing the entry by the Company into a material definitive agreement to acquire the membership
interests of the entities that own 4612 Navistar Drive (&#147;Navistar&#148;) and Metro Park North (&#147;Metro
Park&#148;), an aggregate portfolio of five industrial and flex buildings located in suburban Maryland
totaling approximately 405,000 square feet (collectively, the &#147;Properties&#148;).

<P align="left" style="font-size: 10pt">On December&nbsp;23, 2004, the Company completed the acquisition of the Properties from certain
affiliates of T. Richard Butera and RIP Investments LP. The aggregate purchase price for the
Properties was approximately $63.0&nbsp;million, consisting of the assumption of approximately $37.3
million of mortgage debt on the Properties, with the remainder to be paid in cash from borrowings
under the Company&#146;s revolving credit facility. The following table sets forth certain approximate
information regarding the Properties:

<P align="left" style="font-size: 10pt">The Indebtedness includes certain customary covenants, including without limitation, maintenance of
a minimum net worth by the guarantor, debt coverage requirements, maintaining insurance on the
Properties, providing financial reports and maintenance of certain reserves.

<P align="left" style="font-size: 10pt">The Indebtedness also includes prepayment penalties and customary events of default, including
failure to pay a sum due, entry into a subordinate financing without the lender&#146;s consent and a
change in control of the Company. Upon the occurrence of an event of default under the
Indebtedness, the lender may accelerate the obligation and declare amounts outstanding to be
immediately due and payable and may foreclose on the Properties. A default under the Indebtedness
could also trigger a default under the Company&#146;s line of credit, which could accelerate amounts due
under that agreement as well and result in foreclosures on the Company&#146;s other properties.

<P align="left" style="font-size: 10pt">FIRST POTOMAC REALTY TRUST COMPLETES ACQUISITION OF TWO SUBURBAN MARYLAND PROPERTIES



<P align="left" style="font-size: 10pt"><B><I>Company Completes $272 Million in Acquisitions in 2004</I></B>



<P align="left" style="font-size: 10pt">BETHESDA, Md. &#151; (December&nbsp;28, 2004) &#151; First Potomac Realty Trust (NYSE:FPO), a real estate
investment trust that acquires and operates industrial and flex properties in the Washington, D.C.
metropolitan area and other major markets in Virginia and Maryland, has completed the previously
announced acquisition of the Campus at Metro Park North and 4612 Navistar Drive in suburban
Maryland for $63&nbsp;million.


<P align="left" style="font-size: 10pt">The Campus at Metro Park North in Rockville, Maryland, is a four-building flex property built in
2001 totaling approximately 190,000 square feet. 4612 Navistar Drive is a one-building industrial
property built in 1998 totaling approximately 215,000 square feet. The Navistar Drive property is
located in Frederick, Maryland, directly across the street from the Company&#146;s property at 6900
English Muffin Way and includes additional land that can accommodate another building of
approximately 50,000 square feet. Both properties are currently 100% occupied and are expected to
generate a first-year cash return of approximately 8.1% on the purchase price.


<P align="left" style="font-size: 10pt">The acquisition was financed by the assumption of two separate first mortgages totaling
approximately $37&nbsp;million and borrowings under the Company&#146;s unsecured credit facility. The
weighted average interest rate on the mortgages that were assumed is 7.24%.


<P align="left" style="font-size: 10pt">Commenting on the announcement, Douglas J. Donatelli, First Potomac&#146;s chief executive officer,
stated, &#147;Metro Park North and Navistar Drive allow us to expand our presence in suburban Maryland.
Both properties are in growing markets near other properties in our portfolio. The properties have
strong tenancy and were recently constructed, and we expect them to generate solid returns over
both the short and long term. They also further solidify our already strong portfolio, which is
more than 95% leased, and will allow us to focus on more opportunistic acquisitions in 2005.&#148;

<P align="left" style="font-size: 10pt"><B>About First Potomac Realty Trust</B><BR>

acquires and operates industrial and flex properties in the Washington, D.C. metropolitan area and
other major markets in Virginia and Maryland. The Company owns an 80-building portfolio totaling
approximately 5.3&nbsp;million square feet. The Company&#146;s largest tenant is the U.S. Government, which
leases approximately 687,000 square feet in the Company&#146;s properties under 23 leases.
