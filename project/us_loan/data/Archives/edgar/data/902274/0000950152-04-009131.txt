<P align="left" style="font-size: 10pt"><U><B>Item&nbsp;1.01 Material Definitive Agreement</B></U>



<P align="left" style="margin-left: 0%; text-indent: 0%; margin-right: 0%; font-size: 10pt">On December&nbsp;17, 2004, Libbey Glass Inc. and Libbey Europe B.V., entered into Amendment
No.&nbsp;1 and Waiver to Credit Agreement (&#147;Amendment Agreement&#148;) to amend its Credit Agreement,
dated June&nbsp;24, 2004, among Libbey Glass Inc. and Libbey Europe B.V., as borrowers, the Bank
of America, N.A., as the Administrative Agent, Swing Line Lender and as an L/C Issuer, The
Bank of New York, as the Syndication Agent, The Bank of Tokyo-Mitsubishi, Ltd., Chicago
Branch, as the Documentation Agent, and the other lenders listed therein. Material terms of
the Amendment Agreement are (1)&nbsp;the Offshore Currency Sublimit provided for in the Credit
Agreement has been increased from $100&nbsp;million to $125&nbsp;million and (2)&nbsp;the leverage ratio
with which the borrowers must comply for the quarters ending December&nbsp;31, 2004, and March
31, 2005, has been increased from a maximum of 3.50 to 1.00 to a maximum of 3.75 to 1.00,
respectively. The borrowers have agreed to pay each lender executing the Amendment
Agreement a fee equal to 5 basis points of the applicable lender&#146;s commitment.


<P align="left" style="margin-left: 0%; text-indent: 0%; margin-right: 0%; font-size: 10pt">Additionally, on December&nbsp;21, 2004, Libbey Glass Inc. entered into an Amendment with respect
to the Note Purchase Agreement and Guaranty Agreement, both dated March&nbsp;31, 2003, among
Libbey Glass Inc. and the Purchasers of the Company&#146;s senior notes maturing March&nbsp;31, 2008,
March&nbsp;31, 2010 and March&nbsp;31, 2013. The amendment allows for a short-term increase in the
consolidated leverage ratio with which Libbey Glass Inc. must comply for the quarters ending
December&nbsp;31, 2004, and March&nbsp;31, 2005, from a maximum of 3.50 to 1.00 to a maximum of 3.75
to 1.00, respectively. The borrowers have agreed to pay each lender a fee equal to 5 basis
points of the applicable lender&#146;s commitment.
