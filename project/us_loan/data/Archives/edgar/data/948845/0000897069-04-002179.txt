<H1 ALIGN=CENTER><FONT FACE="Times New Roman, Times, Serif" SIZE=2>JOHN &#147;JACK&#148;WEEKLY
DIES AT AGE 73;<BR>LEAVES LEGACY OF SERVICE TO MIDWEST AIRLINES  </FONT></H1>

<!-- MARKER FORMAT-SHEET="Para Flush Lv 0-TNR" FSL="Project" -->
<P><FONT FACE="Times New Roman, Times, Serif" SIZE=2><B>Milwaukee, Wisconsin, December 20,
2004 &#150; Midwest Air Group (NYSE: MEH) </B>today informed its shareholders that a
long-time member of its board of directors has passed away. John W. &#147;Jack&#148;
Weekly, chairman and chief executive officer of Mutual of Omaha Insurance Company, died
Friday, December 17. Weekly had been hospitalized since falling earlier in the month. He
was 73. </FONT></P>

<!-- MARKER FORMAT-SHEET="Para Flush Lv 0-TNR" FSL="Project" -->
<P><FONT FACE="Times New Roman, Times, Serif" SIZE=2>Weekly had served as a member of the
airline holding company&#146;s board of directors since 1995, when Midwest Airlines became
a publicly held company. In April 2004, he was re-elected to serve a fourth consecutive
three-year term as director. He was also a member of the board&#146;s Compensation
Committee. </FONT></P>

<!-- MARKER FORMAT-SHEET="Para Flush Lv 0-TNR" FSL="Project" -->
<P><FONT FACE="Times New Roman, Times, Serif" SIZE=2>&#147;We were blessed to have the
benefit of Jack&#146;s wisdom and guidance for nearly a decade,&#148; said Timothy E.
Hoeksema, chairman and chief executive officer of Midwest Airlines. &#147;He had the
unique ability to focus on the heart of any issue and quickly determine the best course of
action. He was extremely supportive of Midwest Airlines and our mission to provide a
higher level of customer service to the traveling public. We&#146;ll truly miss his
inspirational leadership.&#148; </FONT></P>

<!-- MARKER FORMAT-SHEET="Para Flush Lv 0-TNR" FSL="Default" -->
<P><FONT FACE="Times New Roman, Times, Serif" SIZE=2>Midwest Airlines features jet service
throughout the United States, including Milwaukee&#146;s most daily nonstop flights and
best schedule to major destinations. Skyway Airlines, Inc. &#150; its wholly owned
subsidiary &#150; operates as Midwest Connect, which offers connections to Midwest
Airlines as well as point-to-point service between select markets on regional jet and
turboprop aircraft. Together, the airlines offer service to 50 cities. More information is
available at <I>www.midwestairlines.com.</I> </FONT></P>
