Item 4.02 Non-Reliance on Previously Issued Financial Statements or a Related Audit Report or Completed Interim Review

In December 2004, Alaska Airlines, a subsidiary of Alaska Air Group, discovered a clerical error in its calculation of deferred Mileage Plan revenue that existed as of September 30, 2004. Management discussed the issue with Alaska Air Group’s Audit Committee and independent auditor, KPMG. To provide investors with timely information, management and the Audit Committee determined on December 20, 2004 to file a Form 10-Q/A for the quarter ended September 30, 2004 to reflect the impact of this item to the Company’s third quarter and year to date reported condensed consolidated financial statements rather than record the adjustment in the fourth quarter. As described in greater detail in the Form 10-Q/A (also filed today), the revision increases total Mileage Plan liability and deferred revenue balance from $379.2 million to $387.6 million and results in an $8.4 million (pretax) decrease to other revenues for the third quarter and nine months ended September 30, 2004.
