<p style="text-indent: 60px"><b><i><font size="3" face="Times New Roman">Acquisition of Beacon Respiratory Services, Inc.</font></i></b></p>

<p style="text-indent: 60px"><font size="3" face="Times New Roman">On December 22, 2004, Arcadia Resources, Inc. (the &ldquo;Company&rdquo;) agreed to
purchase the outstanding common stock of Beacon Respiratory Services, Inc., Beacon Respiratory Services of Alabama, Inc., Beacon
Respiratory Services of Colorado, Inc., and Beacon Respiratory Services of Georgia, Inc.&nbsp; These businesses furnish respiratory
services in Florida, Alabama, Colorado, and Georgia and collectively have revenues of approximately $1.8 million annually.&nbsp; We
also closed the purchase on December 22, 2004, and the transaction is effective January 1, 2005.&nbsp;</font></p>

<p style="text-indent: 60px"><font size="3" face="Times New Roman">The purchase price is $1.5 million, including the payment of outstanding shareholder
notes.&nbsp; The purchase price is subject to increase by up to an additional $400,000 based on changes in reimbursement rates for
calendar year 2005, with any additional amounts owed being payable to the sellers by January 1, 2006.&nbsp; By January 15, 2005,
$993,726 in cash is payable to Beacon&rsquo;s shareholders, less $150,000 to be held by the Company to offset valid indemnification
and other claims of the Company.&nbsp; The Company&rsquo;s counsel will hold 16.33% of&nbsp; the Beacon shares purchased until the
$150,000 (including accrued interest less any amounts disputed) is paid to sellers by January 1, 2006.&nbsp; During this time, the
Company may vote such shares and shall have all incidents of ownership.</font></p>

<p style="text-indent: 60px"><font size="3" face="Times New Roman">By January 15, 2004, one of Beacon&rsquo;s selling shareholders will be issued a
promissory note for $151,274, with a one-year term at 8% annual interest.&nbsp; Principal and interest will be payable
quarterly.&nbsp; If the Company defaults on the note, the selling shareholder may require the Company to transfer back the shares
of stock sold by this selling shareholder (i.e., 33% of the Beacon shares purchased by the Company) or elect other remedies.&nbsp;
These shares will be held by the Company&rsquo;s counsel until the note is paid.&nbsp; Until the note is paid, the Company may vote
such shares and shall have all incidents of ownership.</font></p>

<p><b><font size="3" face="Times New Roman">Item 8.01&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp; Other Events.</font></b></p>

<p style="text-indent: 60px"><font size="3" face="Times New Roman"><b>Acquisition of BK
Tool, Inc.</b></font></p>

<p style="text-indent: 60px"><font size="3" face="Times New Roman">On December 17, 2004, our Grayrose, Inc. subsidiary purchased substantially all of the
assets of BK Tool, Inc., a light industrial staffing business located in Sterling Heights, Michigan, with approximately $2 million
in annual revenues.&nbsp; The purchase price was $175,000 plus 85% of accounts receivables no greater than 60 days
outstanding.&nbsp; At closing, we paid the seller $393,000 in cash, which we financed by drawing on our revolving credit
facility.&nbsp; An additional&nbsp; $75,000 is payable to the seller within 30 days of closing.</font></p>

<p style="text-indent: 60px"><b><i><font size="3" face="Times New Roman">Comerica Bank Credit Agreement</font></i></b></p>

<p style="text-indent: 60px"><font size="3" face="Times New Roman">On May 7, 2004, our Arcadia Services, Inc. subsidiary and three of its wholly-owned
subsidiaries entered into a credit agreement with Comerica Bank which provides the borrowers with a revolving credit facility with
a credit commitment amount of up to $12 million through May 7, 2006.&nbsp; The credit agreement provides that advances to the
Company will not exceed the lesser of the revolving credit commitment amount or the aggregate principal amount of indebtedness
permitted under the advance formula amount at any one time.&nbsp; The advance formula base is 85% of the eligible accounts
receivable, plus the lesser of 85% of eligible unbilled&nbsp; accounts or $2 million, plus an overformula advance of $1
million payable within one year in 12 monthly installments.&nbsp; Except for the initial advance of $11.5 million which was used to finance a portion of the purchase price of the
acquisition of Arcadia Services, Inc. on May 7, 2004, advances on the credit facility are to be used solely for working capital
purposes.&nbsp; Thereafter, on July 29, 2004, the revolving credit commitment amount was
increased to $14.4 million.&nbsp; Subsequently, additional funds were drawn on
the overformula advance.</font></p>
<p style="text-indent: 60px"><font size="3" face="Times New Roman">On November
23, 2004,
the revolving credit commitment amount was increased to $16
million, and the credit agreement was also amended to provide an overformula
balance of $1.2 million payable at the rate of $100,000 per month for 12
consecutive months, beginning in January, 2005.&nbsp; Further, certain amendments were made to relative to accounts from business originating from state Medicaid plans eligible for
inclusion in the advance formula.&nbsp;</font></p>

                            STOCK PURCHASE AGREEMENT

        THIS STOCK PURCHASE AGREEMENT is made and entered into this 22nd day of
December, 2004, effective as of January 1, 2005 (the "Effective Date"), by and
among ARCADIA RESOURCES, INC., a Nevada corporation ("Buyer"), BEACON
RESPIRATORY SERVICES, INC., BEACON RESPIRATORY SERVICES OF ALABAMA, INC., BEACON
RESPIRATORY SERVICES OF COLORADO, INC., BEACON RESPIRATORY SERVICES OF GEORGIA,
INC., (individually a "Company" and collectively the "Companies"), and REBECCA
R. IRISH, B. REID WILBURN, Florida residents, and RYAN T. POWERS, a Georgia
resident (collectively "Sellers"). Certain other capitalized terms used herein
are defined in Article X and throughout this Agreement.


                                R E C I T A L S :

        A. Sellers own 100% of the issued and outstanding shares (the "Shares")
of capital stock of the Companies.

        B. The Companies are engaged in the sale and leasing of durable medical
equipment, including home respiratory and oxygen equipment (the "Business").

        C. The facilities of the Companies consist of the Companies' leased
facilities located at Ocala, Florida; Fultondale, Alabama; Denver, Colorado; and
Atlanta, Georgia (collectively the "Facilities").

        D. Sellers desire to sell, and Buyer desires to purchase, all of the
Shares upon the terms and subject to the conditions, representations, warranties
and covenants contained in this Agreement.

        E. Sellers, as owners of all of the Shares, will benefit from the sale
of the Shares to Buyer and are joining in this Agreement as a material
inducement to Buyer and as a condition to Buyer's willingness to enter into this
Agreement.

                                    AGREEMENT

        NOW, THEREFORE, in consideration of the premises and the mutual
promises, representations, warranties and covenants herein contained, the
parties agree as follows:

                                   ARTICLE I
               SALE AND TRANSFER OF SHARES; CLOSING; OTHER MATTERS

        1.1 SALE AND PURCHASE OF SHARES. Subject to the terms and conditions of
this Agreement, at the Closing, effective as of the Effective Date, Sellers will
sell, convey, assign, deliver and transfer the Shares to Buyer, and Buyer will
purchase the Shares from Sellers free and clear of any and all Encumbrances.

        1.2 PURCHASE PRICE. The purchase price for the Shares will be One
Million Five Hundred Thousand Dollars ($1,500,000.00), consisting of a payment
of Eight Hundred Eighty Eight Thousand and Thirty Seven Dollars ($888,037.00)
Dollars as set forth herein, plus the assumption of shareholder notes described
in Schedule 1.2(a) in the amount of Two Hundred Fifty Six Thousand Nine Hundred
Sixty Three Dollars ($256,963), plus the assumption of certain leases on rental
equipment described in Schedule 1.2(b) in the amount of Two Hundred Thirty
Thousand Dollars ($230,000), plus the assumption of the accounts payable set
forth in Schedule 1.2(c) in the amount of One Hundred Twenty Five Thousand
Dollars ($125,000), subject to adjustment of the above numbers comprising the
$1,500,000.00 Purchase Price as of
