<p style="font-weight:bold;margin:0in 0in .0001pt;page-break-after:auto;"><b><font size="2" face="Times New Roman" style="font-size:10.0pt;">Item 8.01. Other
Events.</font></b></p>

<p style="font-weight:bold;margin:0in 0in .0001pt;page-break-after:auto;"><b><font size="2" face="Times New Roman" style="font-size:10.0pt;">&nbsp;</font></b></p>

<p style="margin:0in 0in .0001pt;text-indent:.25in;"><font size="2" face="Times New Roman" style="font-size:10.0pt;">On December 16, 2004, NSP Holdings L.L.C., the parent
company of Norcross Safety Products L.L.C., and NSP Holdings Capital Corp., a
subsidiary of NSP Holdings L.L.C. (together with NSP Holdings L.L.C., the &#147;issuers&#148;),
entered into a purchase agreement with respect to the sale of $92.0 million of
its 11.75% Senior Pay In Kind Notes due 2012.&#160;
The issuers also intend to sell $8.0 million of the notes directly to
CIVC Partners Fund, L.P., an affiliate of the issuers.&#160; Interest on the notes will be payable at the
issuers&#146; option in cash or payable in kind for the first five years, and
thereafter in cash.&#160; On July 1, 2010, the
issuers will be required to mandatorily redeem, at a redemption price equal to
100% of the principal amount plus accrued and unpaid interest, notes in an
aggregate principal amount sufficient to ensure that the notes are not treated
for tax purposes as &#147;Applicable High Yield Discount Obligations.&#148;&#160; Approximately $62.5 million of the proceeds
will be used to make distributions to NSP Holdings L.L.C.&#146;s equity holders and
the remainder will be used for general corporate purposes.&#160; The offering is expected to close on January
7, 2005.</font></p>

<p style="margin:0in 0in .0001pt;text-indent:.25in;"><font size="2" face="Times New Roman" style="font-size:10.0pt;">&nbsp;</font></p>

<p style="margin:0in 0in .0001pt;text-indent:.25in;"><font size="2" face="Times New Roman" style="font-size:10.0pt;">The notes will not be registered under the Securities
Act of 1933, or any state securities laws.&#160;
Therefore, the notes may not be offered or sold in the United States
absent registration under the Securities Act of 1933 or an applicable exemption
from the registration requirements of the Securities Act of 1933 and any
applicable state securities laws.&#160; This
Form&nbsp;8-K is neither an offer to sell nor a solicitation of any offer to
buy the notes.</font></p>
