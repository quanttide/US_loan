<P align="left" style="font-size: 10pt">Item&nbsp;1.01. Entry into a Material Definitive Agreement.


<P align="left" style="font-size: 10pt">&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;On December&nbsp;22, 2004, Post Apartment Homes, L.P. (the &#147;Operating Partnership&#148;) entered into a
letter agreement related to the Credit Agreement, dated January&nbsp;16, 2004, by and among the
Operating Partnership, Wachovia Bank, National Association, as Administrative Agent, and certain
lenders thereto, as amended (the &#147;Credit Agreement&#148;) and on December&nbsp;23, 2004, the Operating
Partnership entered into a letter agreement with Fannie Mae related to (1)&nbsp;the Master Reimbursement
Agreement, dated as of June&nbsp;1, 1995, between Fannie Mae and the Operating Partnership, as amended
and (2)&nbsp;the Reimbursement Agreement dated as of July&nbsp;1, 1999, between Fannie Mae and the Operating
Partnership, as amended (together, the &#147;Reimbursement Agreements&#148;). The Operating Partnership
entered into the letter agreements in connection with the anticipated termination of the
remarketing agreement for its $100&nbsp;million of 6.85% Mandatory Par Put Remarketed Securities
(&#147;MOPPRS&#148;) due March&nbsp;16, 2015. The Operating Partnership paid Merrill Lynch a total of $10.6
million to terminate the remarketing agreement on December&nbsp;28, 2004, which will result in a charge
equal to approximately $0.25 per diluted share in the fourth quarter of 2004.


<P align="left" style="font-size: 10pt">&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;In accordance with GAAP, if the Operating Partnership had exercised its option to redeem the
MOPPRS from Merrill Lynch on March&nbsp;16, 2005, the scheduled remarketing date for the MOPPRS, the
Operating Partnership would have incurred a &#147;loss on early extinguishment of indebtedness&#148; for the
amount of the additional payment and for the write off of unamortized deferred financing costs.
For purposes of calculating financial covenants under the Credit Agreement and the Reimbursement
Agreements, losses on early extinguishment of indebtedness would be excluded from interest, fixed
charge and debt service coverage ratios and, as a result, would not have impacted the financial
covenants or the measurement of the Operating Partnership&#146;s ability to meet debt service
requirements. However, under GAAP the Operating Partnership&#146;s early termination of the remarketing
option in the fourth quarter of 2004 rather than in March&nbsp;2005 will result in the characterization
and treatment of the charge as additional interest expense rather than as loss on early
extinguishment of indebtedness.


<P align="left" style="font-size: 10pt">&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Under the letter agreements, the requisite lenders under the Credit Agreement and Fannie Mae
acknowledged and agreed that, notwithstanding the Operating Partnership&#146;s early termination of
Merrill Lynch&#146;s remarketing option and the classification under GAAP of the related charges as
described above, the Operating Partnership will treat all such charges as a &#147;loss on early
extinguishment of indebtedness&#148; rather than &#147;interest expense&#148; for purposes of computing its
financial covenants under the Credit Agreement and the Reimbursement Agreements as of December&nbsp;31,
2004 and in future periods, as applicable.


<P align="left" style="font-size: 10pt">&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;The above description of the letter agreements is qualified in its entirety by reference to
the letter agreements attached hereto as Exhibits 10.1 and 10.2, the contents of which are
incorporated into this Item&nbsp;1.01 by reference in their entirety.

<P align="left" style="font-size: 10pt">&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;On March&nbsp;16, 1998, the Borrower issued its $100,000,000 6.85% Mandatory Par Put Remarketed
Securities (&#147;MOPPRS&#148;) due March&nbsp;16, 2015. Under the terms of the MOPPRS, Merrill Lynch, as
remarketing dealer, has the option to repurchase these senior promissory notes from investors at
par on March&nbsp;16, 2005, and remarket them at a price equal to 5.715% <U>plus</U> the Borrower&#146;s
then-applicable credit spread above the 10-year treasury rate (which price would represent an
above-market rate that, based on current interest rates, would be expected to result in a premium
to Merrill Lynch of approximately $12,000,000). Under the remarketing agreement, the Borrower has
the right to redeem the MOPPRS from Merrill Lynch on March&nbsp;16, 2005, by repaying the principal debt
outstanding, <U>plus</U> an additional amount based on the difference between the 5.715% rate and
the spot 10-year treasury rate for the remaining payments due over the 10-year term of the
remarketed senior promissory notes. Today, this additional amount payable to Merrill Lynch for
effective termination of its remarketing option would be approximately $12,000,000.


<P align="left" style="font-size: 10pt">&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;In accordance with GAAP, if the Borrower were to exercise its option to redeem the MOPPRS from
Merrill Lynch on March&nbsp;16, 2005, the Borrower would incur a &#147;loss on early extinguishment of
indebtedness&#148; for the amount of the additional payment and for the write off of unamortized
deferred financing costs. Assuming no change in interest rates between now and March&nbsp;16, 2005,
this additional amount would be approximately $13,000,000 (including approximately $700,000 of
unamortized deferred financing costs). For purposes of calculating financial covenants under the
Credit Agreement, losses on early extinguishment of indebtedness would be excluded from interest
and fixed charge coverage ratios and, as a result, would have no impact on the financial covenants
or the measurement of the Borrower&#146;s ability to meet debt service requirements.

interest and fixed charge coverage ratios and could result in non-compliance with such financial
covenants.

<P align="left" style="font-size: 10pt">&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;We refer to the above-referenced Reimbursement Agreements. Capitalized terms used in this
letter that are defined in the Reimbursement Agreements are used in this letter with the respective
meanings provided for such capitalized terms in the Reimbursement Agreements. By this letter, we
are requesting your agreement as to the treatment of certain charges and related calculation of
certain financial covenants in the Reimbursement Agreements as more particularly described below.


<P align="left" style="font-size: 10pt">&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;On March&nbsp;16, 1998, Post issued its $100,000,000 6.85% Mandatory Par Put Remarketed Securities
(&#147;MOPPRS&#148;) due March&nbsp;16, 2015. Under the terms of the MOPPRS, Merrill Lynch, as remarketing
dealer, has the option to repurchase these senior promissory notes from investors at par on March
16, 2005, and remarket them at a price equal to 5.715% <U>plus</U> Post&#146;s then-applicable credit
spread above the 10-year treasury rate (which price would represent an above-market rate that,
based on current interest rates, would be expected to result in a premium to Merrill Lynch of
approximately $12,000,000). Under the remarketing agreement, Post has the right to redeem the
MOPPRS from Merrill Lynch on March&nbsp;16, 2005, by repaying the principal debt outstanding,
<U>plus</U> an additional amount based on the difference between the 5.715% rate and the spot
10-year treasury rate for the remaining payments due over the 10-year term of the remarketed senior
promissory notes. Today, this additional amount payable to Merrill Lynch for effective termination
of its remarketing option would be approximately $12,000,000.


<P align="left" style="font-size: 10pt">&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;In accordance with GAAP, if Post were to exercise its option to redeem the MOPPRS from Merrill
Lynch on March&nbsp;16, 2005, Post would incur a &#147;loss on early extinguishment of indebtedness&#148; (and,
under GAAP as in effect on the respective dates of the Reimbursement Agreements, would have been
treated as an &#147;extraordinary loss&#148;) for the amount of the additional payment and for the write off
of unamortized deferred financing costs. Assuming no change in interest rates between now and
March&nbsp;16, 2005, this additional amount would be approximately $13,000,000 (including approximately
$700,000 of unamortized deferred financing costs). For purposes of calculating financial covenants
under the Reimbursement Agreements, losses on

<P align="left" style="font-size: 10pt">early extinguishment of indebtedness/extraordinary losses would be excluded from debt service

measurement of Post&#146;s ability to meet debt service requirements.


<P align="left" style="font-size: 10pt">&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Post has determined that it would be in its best interests to terminate Merrill Lynch&#146;s
remarketing option in the fourth quarter of 2004 rather than in March&nbsp;2005. However, this earlier
termination, according to GAAP, would result in the characterization and treatment of this
additional amount as approximately $12,000,000 of additional interest expense and approximately
$700,000 of additional amortization of deferred financing costs&#151;rather than as &#147;loss on early
extinguishment of debt&#148; as described above. Assuming that the forward yield curve accurately
reflects the market&#146;s expectation of future interest rates, however, the economic impact of this
earlier termination is essentially the same as if Post exercised its redemption option on March&nbsp;16,
2005. Rather than Post paying Merrill Lynch $112,000,000 to redeem the MOPPRS on March&nbsp;16, 2005,
Post would pay approximately $12,000,000 to terminate the remarketing option now, and would pay
investors $100,000,000 to redeem the principal amount of the MOPPRS at par on March&nbsp;16, 2005.
Although no different economically (again, assuming that future interest rates are accounted for in
the yield curve), the GAAP characterization of the approximately $12,000,000 payment as interest
expense and the additional $700,000 as amortization of deferred financing costs would presumably be
required to be included in Post&#146;s computation of debt service coverage ratios and could result in
non-compliance with such financial covenants.


<P align="left" style="font-size: 10pt">&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Post does not believe that the 3-month difference in timing of this transaction should alter
the calculation of the financial covenants. Furthermore, Post does not believe that this
non-recurring charge materially impacts its leverage or affects its ability to meet its ongoing
debt service obligations. Accordingly, Post is requesting that Fannie Mae acknowledge and agree
that, notwithstanding Post&#146;s termination of Merrill Lynch&#146;s remarketing option prior to March&nbsp;16,
2005 and the classification under GAAP of the related charges as described above, Post shall treat
all such charges as a &#147;loss on early extinguishment of debt&#148; (and as an &#147;extraordinary loss&#148;)
rather than &#147;interest expense&#148; for purposes of computing its financial covenants under the
Reimbursement Agreements as of December&nbsp;31, 2004 and in future periods as applicable. Please sign
and return a copy of this letter to evidence your acknowledgment and agreement, which shall be
effective as of the date of this letter.

<P align="center" style="font-size: 10pt"><I>Post Terminates Remarketing Agreement Related to its $100&nbsp;million of<BR>
MandatOry Par Put Remarketed Securities (MOPPRS)</I>



<P align="left" style="margin-left: 0%; text-indent: 0%; margin-right: 0%; font-size: 10pt">ATLANTA, December&nbsp;28, 2004 &#150; Post Properties, Inc. (NYSE: PPS) announced today that it has
terminated the remarketing agreement related to its $100&nbsp;million of 6.85% MandatOry Par Put
Remarketed Securities (&#147;MOPPRS&#148;) due March&nbsp;16, 2015. In connection with the termination of
the remarketing agreement, Post will pay Merrill Lynch a total of $10.6&nbsp;million. This will
result in a charge, equal to approximately $0.25 per diluted share, which will be recorded in
the fourth quarter of 2004.


<P align="left" style="margin-left: 0%; text-indent: 0%; margin-right: 0%; font-size: 10pt">The MOPPRS were originally issued by the Company in 1998. Under the terms of the remarketing
agreement Merrill Lynch had the right to remarket the underlying unsecured bonds on the March
16, 2005 remarketing date for a ten-year term and at an interest rate to Post calculated as
5.715% plus Post&#146;s then current credit spread to the ten-year Treasury.


<P align="left" style="margin-left: 0%; text-indent: 0%; margin-right: 0%; font-size: 10pt">In addition to terminating the remarketing agreement, Post expects to pay off the $100&nbsp;million
of unsecured bonds at par on March&nbsp;16, 2005.


<P align="left" style="margin-left: 0%; text-indent: 0%; margin-right: 0%; font-size: 10pt">Said Christopher Papa, Post&#146;s Executive Vice President and Chief Financial Officer, &#147;Based on
current interest rates, the forward Treasury yield curve and our capital plans for 2005, we
have concluded that it is not in the best interest of the Company to permit the MOPPRS debt to
be remarketed for an additional ten-year term at a substantially above-market interest rate.
Having made that decision, we elected to proceed to terminate the remarketing agreement in the
fourth quarter of 2004.&#148;
