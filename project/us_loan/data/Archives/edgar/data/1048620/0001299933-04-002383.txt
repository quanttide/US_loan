<B>
	Item 4.02. Non-Reliance on Previously Issued Financial Statements or a Related Audit Report or Completed Interim Review.
</B>
</FONT>
</P>
<P ALIGN="LEFT">
<FONT SIZE="2">
On December 20, 2004, we received a letter from Schechter Dokken Kanter Andrews & Selcer Ltd., our independent auditor, setting forth its determination that our financial statements for the fiscal year ended December 29, 2002, and all subsequent annual and interim periods, should be restated.  The letter expressed the independent auditor's belief that the accounting for our sale in 2002 of shares of convertible preferred stock with detachable warrants should have included the calculation of a beneficial conversion feature.  This letter, which appears as Exhibit 7 hereto, is incorporated by reference in response to this Item 4.02.<br>The audit committee of our board of directors has discussed with the independent auditor these matters.  As a result of such discussions, we intend to amend the foregoing periodic reports to make the necessary adjustments.<br>We have provided the independent auditor with a copy of the foregoing disclosures and we have requested that the independent auditor furnish to us a letter 
addressed to the U.S. Securities and Exchange Commission stating whether it agrees with the statements made by us in this disclosure.  We intend to amend this report to file such letter no later than two business days after our receipt of such letter.<br>

<P align="left" style="font-size: 11pt">Mr.&nbsp;Steven J. Wagenheim
<BR>

5831 Cedar Lake Road

St. Louis Park, Minnesota 55416


<P align="left" style="font-size: 11pt">Dear Mr.&nbsp;Wagenheim,


<P align="left" style="font-size: 11pt">As you are aware, Granite City received a comment letter from the Securities and Exchange
Commission dated November&nbsp;30, 2004. We have been researching comment number 12 in that letter
relating to the sale in 2002 of shares of convertible preferred stock with detachable warrants. We
have discussed this matter with Monica Underwood and Company legal counsel and presented
preliminary comments to the Company&#146;s Board of Directors on December&nbsp;16, 2004. We have completed
our research and believe that the accounting for this transaction in 2002 should have included the
calculation of a beneficial conversion feature. Monica has calculated the impact of this
accounting difference and has shared it with you and the Board.


<P align="left" style="font-size: 11pt">We believe the effect of this difference on the December&nbsp;29, 2002 and December&nbsp;28, 2003 financial
statements is material. Accordingly, this is to advise Granite City Food &#038; Brewery Ltd. that the
financial statements for the year ended December&nbsp;29, 2002 and all subsequent annual and interim
periods should be restated. Furthermore, the Company should take the appropriate action to prevent
future reliance on our audit report on each of those annual statements as well as the financial
statements for such interim periods.


<P align="left" style="font-size: 11pt">We will work cooperatively with the Company in this matter.


<P align="left" style="font-size: 11pt">Sincerely,
<BR>
Schechter Dokken Kanter
