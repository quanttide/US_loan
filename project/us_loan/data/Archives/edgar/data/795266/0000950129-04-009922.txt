<P align="center" style="font-size: 10pt"><B>KB HOME REPORTS RECORD FOURTH QUARTER AND FULL YEAR 2004 RESULTS<BR>
Fourth Quarter EPS Up 34% to $4.42, Net Orders Up 28%;<BR>
2004 Revenues of $7.05 Billion, Up 21%; EPS of $11.40 Increases 30%<BR>
Backlog Increases 57% to $4.82 Billion; 2005 Earnings Estimate Raised to $14.50</B>


<P align="left" style="font-size: 10pt"><I>Los Angeles, CA, December&nbsp;16, 2004 </I>&#151; KB Home (NYSE: KBH), one of the largest homebuilders in the
United States and France, today announced record financial results for its fourth quarter and
fiscal year ended November&nbsp;30, 2004. Highlights include:



<P>

<TABLE width="100%" border="0" cellpadding="0" cellspacing="0" style="font-size: 10pt">
<TR valign="top" style="font-size: 10pt; color: #000000; background: transparent">
    <TD width="3%" style="background: transparent">&nbsp;</TD>
    <TD width="1%" nowrap align="right">&#149;</TD>
    <TD width="1%">&nbsp;</TD>
    <TD>Company-wide revenues of $7.05&nbsp;billion in 2004 increased 21% from
$5.85&nbsp;billion in 2003. This strong top-line growth was primarily
driven by a 16% increase in unit deliveries to 31,646 in 2004 from
27,331 in 2003. The Company&#146;s 2004 net income totaled $480.9
million, up 30% from $370.8&nbsp;million in 2003. Diluted earnings per
share also rose 30% to $11.40 in 2004 from $8.80 in 2003.</TD>
</TR>

</TABLE>


<P>

<TABLE width="100%" border="0" cellpadding="0" cellspacing="0" style="font-size: 10pt">
<TR valign="top" style="font-size: 10pt; color: #000000; background: transparent">
    <TD width="3%" style="background: transparent">&nbsp;</TD>
    <TD width="1%" nowrap align="right">&#149;</TD>
    <TD width="1%">&nbsp;</TD>
    <TD>During the fourth quarter, KB Home delivered 10,285 homes,
representing a 16% increase from the 8,874 homes delivered in the
year-earlier period. Revenues in the fourth quarter reached $2.38
billion, up 27% from $1.87&nbsp;billion in the fourth quarter of 2003
driven by unit delivery growth and an 11% increase in the Company&#146;s
average selling price. Net income rose 35% to $186.7&nbsp;million in the
fourth quarter of 2004 from $138.7&nbsp;million in the corresponding
quarter of 2003 largely due to higher unit volume and an expanded
operating margin. Diluted earnings per share increased 34% to $4.42
in the quarter ended November&nbsp;30, 2004, up from $3.31 in the
year-earlier quarter.</TD>
</TR>

</TABLE>


<P>

<TABLE width="100%" border="0" cellpadding="0" cellspacing="0" style="font-size: 10pt">
<TR valign="top" style="font-size: 10pt; color: #000000; background: transparent">
    <TD width="3%" style="background: transparent">&nbsp;</TD>
    <TD width="1%" nowrap align="right">&#149;</TD>
    <TD width="1%">&nbsp;</TD>
    <TD>Net orders in the fourth quarter of 2004 rose 28% to 8,516, up from
6,629 net orders in the year-earlier quarter. Underscoring the
Company&#146;s growth in existing markets and extended reach into new
markets through de novo growth and acquisitions, net orders grew in
nearly all of KB Home&#146;s U.S. geographic regions and in France. The
Company&#146;s year-over-year net order growth exceeded 20% in every
quarter of 2004 and reached its highest level for the year during the
fourth quarter.</TD>
</TR>

</TABLE>


<P>

<TABLE width="100%" border="0" cellpadding="0" cellspacing="0" style="font-size: 10pt">
<TR valign="top" style="font-size: 10pt; color: #000000; background: transparent">
    <TD width="3%" style="background: transparent">&nbsp;</TD>
    <TD width="1%" nowrap align="right">&#149;</TD>
    <TD width="1%">&nbsp;</TD>
    <TD>KB Home&#146;s backlog at November&nbsp;30, 2004, in both units and dollar
value, was the highest of any year-end in the Company&#146;s history,
driven by substantial growth in all geographic regions. The dollar
value of the Company&#146;s backlog at November&nbsp;30, 2004 rose 57% from
year-end 2003 to approximately $4.82&nbsp;billion, representing a strong
pipeline of future revenues for 2005. Unit backlog at November&nbsp;30,
2004 stood at 20,280, representing a 38% increase from 14,675 units
at November&nbsp;30, 2003.</TD>
</TR>


</TABLE>

<P align="left" style="font-size: 10pt">&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&#147;KB Home&#146;s operating and financial performance throughout 2004 was nothing short of
outstanding,&#148; said Bruce Karatz, the Company&#146;s chairman and chief executive officer. &#147;We delivered
a record 31,646 homes, generating more than $7&nbsp;billion in revenues and $480&nbsp;million in net income,
both double-digit improvements over 2003. What&#146;s more, this performance fits a broader pattern of
success for the Company. Our revenues and earnings per share over the past five years have
increased at compound annual rates of 13% and 30%, respectively, underscoring the impact of our
geographic and product diversification and demonstrating our ability to execute a disciplined
growth strategy.&#148;


<P align="left" style="font-size: 10pt">&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Total revenues in the fourth quarter of 2004 rose 27% to $2.38&nbsp;billion, up from $1.87&nbsp;billion
in the fourth quarter of 2003, reflecting strength in the Company&#146;s core homebuilding business.
Housing revenues increased 28% in the quarter to $2.36&nbsp;billion, up from $1.84&nbsp;billion in the
year-earlier quarter, due to increases in unit volume and the average selling price in the
Company&#146;s operations in the U.S. and France. Company-wide home deliveries reached 10,285 in the
fourth quarter of 2004, a 16% increase from 8,874 unit deliveries in the final quarter of 2003
while the Company&#146;s overall average selling price rose 11% to $229,200 from $207,400.


<P align="left" style="font-size: 10pt">&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&#147;Each of our five homebuilding regions posted double-digit increases in fourth-quarter
revenues boosting Company-wide results to an all-time record for the period,&#148; noted Karatz. This
performance has increased our confidence in the Company&#146;s ability to capitalize on market
