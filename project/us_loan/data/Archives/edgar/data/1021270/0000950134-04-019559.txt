<P align="left" style="font-size: 10pt"><B>Item&nbsp;2.03. Triggering Events That Accelerate or Increase a Direct Financial
Obligation or an Obligation under an Off-Balance Sheet Transaction</B>.



<P align="left" style="font-size: 10pt">&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;As mentioned in the registrant&#146;s quarterly report on Form 10-Q for the fiscal quarter ended
September&nbsp;26, 2004, a subsidiary of the registrant was named as a defendant in a lawsuit filed in
the Court of Common Pleas, Warren County, Ohio. The lawsuit relates to, among other things, various
alleged defaults by a franchisee of the registrant under its Middletown, Ohio lease and defaults by
the registrant&#146;s subsidiary under a guaranty agreement pursuant to which the subsidiary guaranteed
the franchisee&#146;s performance under the lease. Based on correspondence from the landlord recently
received by the registrant and a recently amended complaint, the landlord is seeking damages in an
amount up to approximately $1.6&nbsp;million. The Company is in the very preliminary stages of
investigating the landlord&#146;s claim and has identified several factors and potential defenses to the
landlord&#146;s claim that the registrant believes may mitigate the registrant&#146;s liability materially or
altogether. The registrant intends to defend vigorously against the claim.
