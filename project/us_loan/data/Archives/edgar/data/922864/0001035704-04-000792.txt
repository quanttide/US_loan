<P align="left" style="font-size: 10pt"><B>Item&nbsp;1.01 Entry into a Material Definitive Agreement.</B>


<P align="left" style="font-size: 10pt">&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Apartment Investment and Management Company (the &#147;Company&#148;) entered into an Underwriting
Agreement, dated as of December&nbsp;16, 2004 (the &#147;Underwriting Agreement&#148;), by and among the Company,
AIMCO Properties, L.P. (the &#147;Operating Partnership&#148;), and Deutsche Bank Securities Inc. and UBS
Securities LLC, as managers for the underwriters named therein (collectively, the &#147;Underwriters&#148;).
Pursuant to the Underwriting Agreement, the Company agreed to sell to the Underwriters 3,000,000 shares of its 7.875% Class&nbsp;Y
Cumulative Preferred Stock, $.01 par value per share (the &#147;Class&nbsp;Y Preferred Stock&#148;), at a price
of $24.2125 per share, for resale to the public by the Underwriters at $25 per share. The Company also gave the Underwriters an option to purchase up to 450,000
additional shares of the Class&nbsp;Y Preferred Stock to cover
over-allotments. The over-allotment option has been exercised in full. The closing of the
sale of the 3,450,000 shares of Class Y Preferred Stock contemplated by the Underwriting Agreement occurred on December&nbsp;21, 2004.
A copy of the Underwriting Agreement is filed as Exhibit&nbsp;1.1 to this report and incorporated herein
by reference.


<P align="left" style="font-size: 10pt"><B>Item&nbsp;5.03 Amendment to Articles of Incorporation or Bylaws.</B>


<P align="left" style="font-size: 10pt">&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;On December&nbsp;17, 2004, the Company filed with the State of Maryland
Articles Supplementary relating to the Class&nbsp;Y Preferred Stock (the &#147;Class&nbsp;Y Articles&#148;), which Articles Supplementary were
effective on filing. The Class&nbsp;Y Articles will classify 3,450,000 authorized but unissued
shares of the Company&#146;s Class&nbsp;A Common Stock into 3,450,000 shares of Class&nbsp;Y Preferred Stock. The
Class&nbsp;Y Preferred Stock entitles the holders thereof to cumulative cash dividends payable
quarterly in an amount per share equal to $0.49219 per quarter. The liquidation preference for the
Class&nbsp;Y Preferred Stock is $25 per share, plus an amount equal to accumulated, accrued and
unpaid dividends, whether or not earned or declared. The Class&nbsp;Y Preferred Stock is not
redeemable prior to December&nbsp;21, 2009, except in limited circumstances relating to the ownership
limitation necessary to preserve the Company&#146;s qualification as a REIT. On and after December&nbsp;21,
2009, the Company will be able to redeem the Class&nbsp;Y Preferred Stock for cash, in whole or from
time to time in part, at a price per share equal to the liquidation preference plus accumulated,
accrued and unpaid dividends, if any, to the redemption date. The Class&nbsp;Y Preferred Stock
ranks prior to the Class&nbsp;A Common Stock of the Company, and on the same level as the Company&#146;s
remaining outstanding shares of preferred stock, with respect to the payment of dividends and the
distributions upon liquidation, dissolution or winding up. Holders of shares of the Class&nbsp;Y
Preferred Stock generally do not have any voting rights. If, however, the Company has not paid
dividends on the Class&nbsp;Y Preferred Stock or any other series of preferred stock for six or more
quarterly periods, whether or not consecutive, holders of the Class&nbsp;Y Preferred Stock, together
with holders of other classes of preferred stock of the Company with similar rights, will be
entitled to elect two additional directors to the Company&#146;s board of directors until all unpaid
dividends on the Class&nbsp;Y Preferred Stock and such other series of preferred stock have been paid or
declared and set apart for payment and dividends thereon for the current quarterly period have been
declared and paid or declared and set apart for payment. Certain material adverse changes to the
terms of the Class&nbsp;Y Preferred Stock cannot be made without the affirmative vote of at least
66-2/3% of the outstanding shares of Class&nbsp;Y Preferred Stock. A copy of the Class&nbsp;Y Articles is
filed as Exhibit&nbsp;4.1 to this report and incorporated herein by reference.

<P align="left" style="font-size: 10pt"><B>Item&nbsp;8.01 Other Events.</B>


<P align="left" style="font-size: 10pt">&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;On December&nbsp;22, 2004, the Company called for redemption 1.25&nbsp;million shares (with a
liquidation preference of $31.25&nbsp;million) of the 8.75% Class&nbsp;D Cumulative Preferred Stock (&#147;Class&nbsp;D
Preferred Stock&#148;) (NYSE: AIVPrD; CUSIP 03748R-30-9). This redemption will be made on January&nbsp;21,
2005 at $25 per share plus all accrued and unpaid dividends up to and including the redemption
date of $0.0425 per share. The redemption price is payable only in cash. This redemption is funded with a
portion of the proceeds of the public offering of the Class&nbsp;Y Preferred Stock. After the January
21, 2005 redemption, no shares of the Class&nbsp;D Preferred Stock will remain outstanding.


<P align="left" style="font-size: 10pt">&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;In addition, on December&nbsp;22, 2004, the Company called for redemption all of its issued and
outstanding 6 1/2% Convertible Subordinated Debentures due 2016 (the &#147;Debentures&#148;) (CUSIP 45767CAB7),
of which $19,643,000 aggregate principal amount is outstanding, and the corresponding 6 1/2% Trust
Convertible Preferred Securities due 2016 (the &#147;Trust Preferred Securities&#148;) (CUSIP
449516301/449516202), and 6 1/2% Trust Convertible Common Securities
due 2016 (&#147;Trust Common Securities&#148; and, together with the
Trust Preferred Securities, the &#147;Trust Securities&#148;), of which $15,019,250 aggregate liquidation
amount of Trust Preferred Securities is outstanding and $4,623,750
aggregate liquidation amount of Trust Common Securities is outstanding.
The Debentures and Trust Securities will be redeemed on
January&nbsp;11, 2005.
All of the Trust Preferred Securities are held by public investors, and all of the Trust Common
Securities are held by the Company. The proceeds from the original issuance of the Trust
Securities by IFT Financing I (formerly known as Insignia Financing I) (the &#147;Trust&#148;) were used to
purchase a corresponding aggregate principal amount of Debentures, which are the sole assets of the
Trust. Accordingly, upon the redemption of the Debentures, pursuant
to the declaration of trust
governing the Trust Securities, the Trust, as the sole holder of the Debentures, will apply the
redemption proceeds of $19,643,000, plus accrued and unpaid distributions, from the Debentures to redeem the outstanding $15,019,250 of
Trust Preferred Securities held by public investors and the $4,623,750 of Trust Common Securities
held by the Company.
The Company assumed the obligations under the
Debentures and the Trust Securities in connection with the Insignia merger in 1998.
The redemption price for each
Trust Security is $50 per security, plus accrued and
unpaid distributions through the redemption date. As an alternative to the cash
redemption, holders of the Trust Securities have the option to convert their securities
into Class&nbsp;A Common Stock of the Company at a conversion rate of $49.61 per security (1.007 shares
per $50 liquidation amount) at any time before 5:00 p.m., New York City time, on January&nbsp;10, 2005.
These redemptions are funded with a portion of the proceeds of the public offering of the Class&nbsp;Y
Preferred Stock. After the January&nbsp;11, 2005 redemption, no Debentures or Trust Securities will remain outstanding.



<P align="left" style="font-size: 10pt">&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;
On December&nbsp;22, 2004, the Company issued a press release announcing the redemption of the Class&nbsp;D
Preferred Stock and the closing of the issuance of the Class&nbsp;Y Preferred Stock. A copy of the
press release is filed as Exhibit&nbsp;99.1 to this report and incorporated herein by reference.

<P align="left" style="font-size: 10pt">DENVER<B>, </B>COLORADO, December&nbsp;22, 2004

<P align="left" style="font-size: 10pt">Apartment Investment and Management Company (&#147;Aimco&#148;) (NYSE:AIV) announced the completion yesterday
of a public offering of 3.45&nbsp;million shares of newly issued 7.875% Class&nbsp;Y Cumulative Preferred
Stock (&#147;Class&nbsp;Y&#148;) for gross proceeds of $86.25&nbsp;million. The Class&nbsp;Y was sold to the public at a
price equal to $25.00 per share and is redeemable at Aimco&#146;s option on or after December&nbsp;21, 2009.
A listing application for the Class&nbsp;Y has been filed with the New York Stock Exchange, and Aimco
expects the Class&nbsp;Y will be approved for listing within 30&nbsp;days.

<P align="left" style="font-size: 10pt">Proceeds from this offering will be used by Aimco to:
