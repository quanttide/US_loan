<P align="left" style="font-size: 10pt"><B>Item&nbsp;1.01 Entry into a Material Definitive Agreement.</B>


<P align="left" style="font-size: 10pt">&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;On December&nbsp;16, 2004, the Lenders (as hereinafter defined) approved the Fourth Amendment to
Credit Agreement (the &#147;Fourth Amendment&#148;) dated as of December&nbsp;6, 2004 by and among Reliance Steel
&#038; Aluminum Co. and RSAC Management Corp. (collectively, &#147;Borrowers&#148;) and the financial institutions
listed on the signature pages thereof (the &#147;Lenders&#148;) and Bank of America, N.A., as administrative
agent for the Lenders (the &#147;Administrative Agent&#148;), and, for purposes of Section&nbsp;5 thereof, the
Guarantors listed on the signature pages thereof. The Fourth Amendment relates to that certain
Credit Agreement dated as of October&nbsp;24, 2001, as amended by the First Amendment to Credit
Agreement dated as of April&nbsp;1, 2002, the Second Amendment to Credit Agreement dated as of February
19, 2003, and the Third Amendment to Credit Agreement dated as of July&nbsp;1, 2003 (as amended, the
&#147;Credit Agreement&#148;), by and among the Borrowers, Lenders and Administrative Agent. The Credit
Agreement allowed Borrowers to declare and pay dividends to the shareholders of Reliance Steel &#038;
Aluminum Co. up to a maximum amount of $2,500,000 in any quarter. The Fourth Amendment, which is
effective as of July&nbsp;1, 2004, increased the maximum amount allowable for dividends in any quarter
to $3,000,000.
