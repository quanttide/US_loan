<P align="left" style="font-size: 10pt">&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;SETO Holdings, Inc. (the &#147;Company&#148;) has received notice from Slamdance Media Group LLC, in
which the Company agreed on July&nbsp;5, 2004 to invest a total of $500,000, that the Company is in
default of its obligations to invest an aggregate of $150,000 due and payable at the rate of
$50,000 on each of October&nbsp;15, November&nbsp;15 and December&nbsp;15, 2004. This investment was carried on
the Company&#146;s balance sheet as of October&nbsp;31, 2004 at a value of $500,000, which as a result of the
notice of default is impaired according to FASB Statement Number 144. The Company may lose its
entire investment in Slamdance if it fails to cure this default, and the Company currently does not
have the ability to make the overdue payments to Slamdance.

<P align="left" style="font-size: 10pt">FOR IMMEDIATE RELEASE



<P align="center" style="font-size: 10pt"><B>SETO HOLDINGS FILES FORM 15 WITH THE SECURITIES AND EXCHANGE<BR>
COMMISSION TERMINATION OF REGISTRATION SUSPENDING ITS DUTY<BR>
TO FILE REPORTS WITH THE SECURITIES AND EXCHANGE<BR>
COMMISSION AND TO DEREGISTER ITS COMMON STOCK</B>


<P align="left" style="font-size: 10pt">Briarcliff Manor, New York, December&nbsp;21, 2004 &#151; SETO Holdings, Inc. (SETO.BB) reported that it had
filed with the Securities and Exchange Commission its Form&nbsp;15 notice of suspension of its duty to
file reports and termination of the registration of its Common Stock under the Securities Exchange
Act of 1934. The Company expects the termination of registration to become effective within 90
days of the Form&nbsp;15 filing with the SEC. However, as a result of the Form&nbsp;15 filing, the Company&#146;s
obligation to file with the SEC certain reports and forms, including Forms10-K, 10-Q and 8-K, have
currently ceased. On or about December&nbsp;22, 2004, the Company&#146;s Common Stock will be delisted from
and will no longer trade on the OTC Bulletin Board. The Company expects that its Common Stock will
trade through the Pink Sheets&#174;, where currently there are 11 brokers listed as market makers,
although no assurance can be given that any broker will continue to make a market in the stock.
The &#147;Pink Sheets&#148; is a centralized quotation service that collects and publishes market maker
quotes in real time, primarily through its website, www.pinksheets.com, which provides stock and
bond price quotes, financial news and information about securities traded.


<P align="left" style="font-size: 10pt">Eugene J. Pian, the Company&#146;s Chief Executive Officer, said that given the lack of analyst coverage
and the minimal liquidity for the Company&#146;s Common Stock, the advantages of continuing as a
reporting company are far outweighed by the disadvantages, particularly the legal and accounting
costs associated with the preparation and filing of the Company&#146;s periodic reports and compliance
with the Sarbanes-Oxley Act of 2002 and the relating regulations. Deregistration will result in a
significant reduction in legal and accounting expenses, and the Company will reallocate these
moneys to its operations. The Company and its shareholders will be better off over the long run as
Management focuses its attention and the Company&#146;s limited financial resources on the Company&#146;s
business, said Mr.&nbsp;Pian.

<P align="center" style="font-size: 10pt"><B>SETO Holdings, Inc. Receives Notice of Default under<BR>
Slamdance Media Group Funding Agreement</B>


<P align="left" style="font-size: 10pt">BRIARCLIFF MANOR, N.Y. , December&nbsp;16, 2004 &#151; SETO Holdings, Inc. (OTCBB-SETO.OB) said that it
received a letter from counsel to Slamdance Media Group LLC claiming that SETO had breached the
provisions of a July&nbsp;5, 2004 agreement requiring SETO to pay an aggregate of $150,000 to Slamdance
at the rate of $50,000 on each of October&nbsp;15, November&nbsp;15 and December&nbsp;15, 2004 (the fourth and
final $50,000 installment being due on January&nbsp;15, 2005). Slamdance also stated that under the
agreement SETO&#146;s ownership interest in Slamdance does not vest at all unless and until SETO pays in
full its $500,000 investment obligation, of which SETO has paid $300,000 to date; therefore
Slamdance maintains that SETO currently has no ownership interest in Slamdance. SETO, which
currently does not have the ability to make any such payments due to the recent reduction of its
line of credit, believes it was legally justified in not making these payments, and SETO intends to
vigorously defend itself.
