<P align="left" style="font-size: 10pt"><B>Item&nbsp;1.01 Entry into a Material Definitive Agreement</B>

<P align="left" style="font-size: 10pt">On December&nbsp;21, 2004, Packeteer, Inc., a Delaware corporation (&#147;Packeteer&#148;) entered into an
Agreement and Plan of Reorganization (the &#147;Agreement&#148;) by and among Packeteer, P Acquisition
Corporation, a California corporation and wholly-owned subsidiary of Packeteer (&#147;Merger Sub&#148;),
Mentat, Inc., a California corporation (&#147;Mentat&#148;), and certain shareholders of Mentat.


<P align="left" style="font-size: 10pt">Pursuant to the Agreement, Mentat and Merger Sub shall combine into a single company through the
statutory merger of Merger Sub with and into Mentat, with Mentat as the surviving
corporation. The Agreement provides, among other things, that the outstanding shares of Mentat&#146;s
Common Stock shall be converted into the right to receive approximately $16.5&nbsp;million in cash. In
addition, Packeteer will pay approximately $3.5&nbsp;million in retention bonuses to Mentat employees in
both cash and restricted stock, with the majority being paid over the next two years.


<P align="left" style="font-size: 10pt">Mentat and Packeteer have made customary representations, warranties and covenants in the
Agreement.


<P align="left" style="font-size: 10pt">A copy of the press release announcing the Agreement is attached hereto as Exhibit&nbsp;99.1 and
incorporated herein by reference.


<!-- link2 "Item&nbsp;2.01 Acquisition or Disposition of Assets" -->

<P align="left" style="font-size: 10pt"><B>Item&nbsp;2.01 Acquisition or Disposition of Assets</B>

<P align="left" style="font-size: 10pt">On December&nbsp;22, 2004, Packeteer, Inc., announced that, through its wholly owned subsidiary, P
Acquisition Corporation, it has completed the acquisition of Mentat, Inc. a privately held company
in Los Angeles, California, as described in Item&nbsp;1.01 above. Mentat is a technology leader in
protocol acceleration technologies and transparent proxies, providing high-performance networking
solutions for satellite and high-latency network links. The acquisition extends Packeteer&#146;s
intellectual property and provides advanced acceleration capabilities for new WAN performance
solutions for global enterprise customers.


<P align="left" style="font-size: 10pt">As described in Item&nbsp;1.01 above, under the terms of the agreement, Packeteer will pay approximately
$16.5&nbsp;million in cash to acquire all the outstanding shares of Mentat. In addition, Packeteer will
pay approximately $3.5&nbsp;million in retention bonuses to Mentat employees in both cash and restricted
stock, with the majority being paid over the next two years.


<P align="left" style="font-size: 10pt">Packeteer will fund the cash portion of the initial proceeds from its current investments.


<P align="left" style="font-size: 10pt">Neither Mentat nor its shareholders have any prior relationship with Packeteer, Inc.

<P align="center" style="font-size: 10pt"><B>PACKETEER<FONT style="font-family: Symbol"><sup>&#210;</sup></FONT>
ACQUIRES LEADING HIGH-PERFORMANCE NETWORKING COMPANY</B>



<P align="center" style="font-size: 10pt"><I>Acquisition expands Packeteer&#146;s Fast WAN vision by incorporating Mentat for accelerated<BR>
applications over satellite and long-haul networks</I>


<P align="left" style="font-size: 10pt"><B>CUPERTINO, Calif. &#151; December&nbsp;22, 2004 &#151; </B>Packeteer<FONT style="font-family: Symbol">&#210;</FONT>, Inc. (NASDAQ: PKTR), the
pioneer and global leader in WAN Application Traffic Management, today announced that it has
acquired privately held Mentat Inc. of Los Angeles, Calif. Mentat is a technology leader in
protocol acceleration technologies and transparent proxies providing high-performance networking
solutions for satellite and high-latency network links. The acquisition deepens and extends
Packeteer&#146;s intellectual property and provides advanced acceleration capabilities for new WAN
performance solutions for global enterprise customers.


<P align="left" style="font-size: 10pt">Under the terms of the agreement, Packeteer will pay approximately $16.5&nbsp;million in cash to acquire
all outstanding shares of Mentat. In addition, Packeteer will pay approximately $3.5&nbsp;million in
retention bonuses to Mentat employees in both cash and restricted stock, with the majority being
paid over the next two years. Mentat will operate as a Packeteer business unit reporting to Mike
Schumacher, VP of Engineering for Packeteer.


<P align="left" style="font-size: 10pt">&#147;This acquisition is an important step in more fully defining our network system vision for Fast
WAN appliances and it provides significant technology for additional acceleration capabilities that
make us more strategic to enterprise networks,&#148; said Dave C&#244;t&#233;, president and CEO of Packeteer.
&#147;In addition to the immediate synergies that we gain in the marketplace, Mentat&#146;s technology
provides a foundation for increasing returns as we consolidate more capabilities into one
appliance.&#148;


<P align="left" style="font-size: 10pt">Mentat brings unique expertise in computer networking to satellite and long-haul links where
Mentat&#146;s award-winning SkyX<sup>&#174;</sup> product line overcomes the limitations of TCP/IP to allow
high-performance network access. Network service providers, corporations and government
organizations throughout the world rely on the SkyX products for high-performing networks with
great user experience.


<P align="left" style="font-size: 10pt">Kay Guyer, Mentat&#146;s CEO stated, &#147;We&#146;re excited to join a company that shares our vision and is
driving this explosive market. Our customers have been requesting more WAN performance and
optimization features such as QoS, monitoring, and central management. With Packeteer, we can now
deliver the most complete solution for WAN performance and optimization to the market.&#148;


<P align="left" style="font-size: 10pt">Dave C&#244;t&#233;, president and CEO, and David Yntema, Chief Financial Officer, will host a conference
call at 11:00 am EDT on Wednesday, December&nbsp;22, 2004, to discuss the acquisition of Mentat. The
dial-in number is 888-316-9415 and the pass code is PACKETEER. A replay of the live conference
call will be available until December&nbsp;29, 2004. To access the replay, the dial-in number is
800-839-9137 and the pass code is 2303.

other acquisition related expenses. Packeteer will provide additional details on the acquisition
during its regularly scheduled year-end financial conference call planned for January&nbsp;27, 2005.


<P align="left" style="font-size: 10pt">Viant Capital of San Francisco acted as financial advisor to Packeteer.


<P align="left" style="font-size: 10pt"><B>About Mentat</B><BR>
Mentat is the leading supplier of standards-based, high-performance networking solutions to ISPs,
enterprises, and government organizations in over 90 countries across all seven continents. More
information can be found at www.mentat.com.


<P align="left" style="font-size: 10pt"><B>About Packeteer</B><BR>
Packeteer<sup>&#174;</sup>, Inc., (NASDAQ: PKTR) is the global market leader in Application Traffic Management for
wide area networks. Deployed at more than 7,000 companies in 50 countries, Packeteer solutions
empower IT organizations with patented network visibility, control, and acceleration capabilities
delivered through a family of intelligent, scalable appliances. For more information, contact
Packeteer at &#043;1 (408)&nbsp;873-4400 or visit the company&#146;s web site at www.packeteer.com.
