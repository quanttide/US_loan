of interest payable on the notes issued pursuant to this Pricing Supplement will be linked to changes in the Consumer Price Index. The Consumer Price Index for purposes of the notes is the non-seasonally adjusted U.S. City Average All Items Consumer
Price Index for All Urban Consumers (&#147;CPI&#148;), published monthly by the Bureau of Labor Statistics of the U.S. Department of Labor (&#147;BLS&#148;) and reported on Bloomberg CPURNSA or any successor service. </FONT></P> <P

the CPI. </B></FONT></P> <P STYLE="margin-top:0px;margin-bottom:-6px"><FONT SIZE="1">&nbsp;</FONT></P> <P STYLE="margin-top:0px;margin-bottom:0px; text-indent:4%"><FONT FACE="Times New Roman" SIZE="2">Your interest payments will be affected by
changes in the CPI. Such changes may be significant. Changes in the CPI are a function of the changes in specified consumer prices over time, which result from the interaction of many factors over which we have no control. </FONT></P> <P
STYLE="margin-top:0px;margin-bottom:0px"><FONT SIZE="1">&nbsp;</FONT></P> <P STYLE="margin-top:0px;margin-bottom:0px"><FONT FACE="Times New Roman" SIZE="2"><B>The Interest Rate on the Notes may be less than the Spread and, in Some Cases, Could be
Zero. </B></FONT></P> <P STYLE="margin-top:0px;margin-bottom:-6px"><FONT SIZE="1">&nbsp;</FONT></P> <P STYLE="margin-top:0px;margin-bottom:0px; text-indent:4%"><FONT FACE="Times New Roman" SIZE="2">Interest payable on the notes is linked to changes
in the level of the CPI during twelve-month measurement periods. If the CPI does not increase during a relevant measurement period, which is likely to occur when there is little or no inflation, holders of the notes will receive interest payments
for that interest period at an interest rate equal to 1.36%, which is referred to as the Spread. If the CPI decreases during a relevant period, which is likely to occur when there is deflation, holders of the notes will receive interest payments for
that period at an interest rate that is less than the Spread. In some cases, holders of the notes could receive only the minimum interest rate, which is 0.00%. </FONT></P> <P STYLE="margin-top:0px;margin-bottom:0px"><FONT SIZE="1">&nbsp;</FONT></P>

reduced. If the CPI is substantially altered, as discussed in greater detail below, a substitute index will be employed to calculate the interest payable on the notes. </FONT></P> <P STYLE="margin-top:0px;margin-bottom:0px"><FONT

notes. Even though the long-term trend in CPI changes has been positive, at any future date, the interest rate on the notes may be below what we would expect to pay as of such date if we issued non-callable senior debt securities with a fixed or
