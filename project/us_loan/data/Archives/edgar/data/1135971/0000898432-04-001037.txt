<P style="margin:0pt; font-family:Times New Roman">Item 2.01 &nbsp;Completion of Acquisition or Disposition of Assets</P>
<P style="margin:0pt; font-family:Times New Roman"><BR></P>
<P style="margin-top:0pt; margin-bottom:10pt; text-indent:36pt; font-family:Times New Roman">On December 21, 2004, Pepco Holdings, Inc. (&#147;PHI&#148;), through its wholly owned subsidiary, Pepco Communications, L.L.C. (&#147;Pepcom&#148;), completed the sale of Pepcom&#146;s 50% membership interest in Starpower Communications, LLC (&#147;Starpower&#148;). &nbsp;Pepcom sold its Starpower membership interest for $29 million in cash to RCN Telecom Services of Washington, D.C., Inc. (&#147;RCN&#148;), a wholly owned subsidiary of RCN Corporation that owns the other 50% interest in Starpower. &nbsp;Starpower provides cable and telecommunication services to households in the Washington, D.C. area. &nbsp;&nbsp;RCN Corporation is a publicly held telecommunications company with headquarters in New Jersey. </P>
<P style="margin-top:0pt; margin-bottom:10pt; text-indent:36pt; font-family:Times New Roman">As previously reported, RCN elected to purchase Pepcom&#146;s Starpower membership interest under a &#147;right of first refusal&#148; provision in the Starpower operating agreement between RCN and Pepcom and, as a result of RCN&#146;s election, Pepcom will pay a $1 million &#147;break-up&#148; fee to the third party with which Pepcom entered into a Starpower membership interest purchase agreement in July 2004. &nbsp;</P>
<P style="margin-top:0pt; margin-bottom:10pt; text-indent:36pt; font-family:Times New Roman">Pepcom and RCN formed Starpower as a joint venture in 1997, and each owned a 50% membership interest in Starpower from that time until the completion of this transaction. &nbsp;With the completion of this transaction, neither Pepcom nor any other affiliate of PHI has any ownership interest in or management responsibility with respect to Starpower.</P>
