<P ALIGN=LEFT><FONT FACE="Times New Roman, Times, Serif" SIZE=2>ITEM 4.01 CHANGES IN
REGISTRANT&#146;S CERTIFYING ACCOUNTANT </FONT></P>

<!-- MARKER FORMAT-SHEET="Para Large Indent" FSL="Default" -->
<P><FONT FACE="Times New Roman, Times, Serif" SIZE=2>&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;On
December 19, 2004, the Board of Directors of Kolorfusion International Inc., a Colorado
corporation (the &#147;Company&#148;), approved and authorized the termination of the
services of Virchow, Krause &amp; Company, LLP (&#147;VK&amp;Co.&#148;) as principal
independent accountant for the Company. The termination of the services of VK&amp;Co. was
a result of communication between the Company and VK&amp;Co. dated December 16, 2004, and the subsequent decision
by the Board of Directors of the Company that it would be in the best interests of the
Company to effect a change in auditors in order to address the needs of a small business
development company. During the Company&#146;s two most recent fiscal years and any
subsequent interim period preceding the resignation of VK&amp;Co., there were no
disagreements with VK&amp;Co. which were not resolved on any matter concerning accounting
principles or practices, financial statement disclosure, or auditing scope or procedure,
which disagreements, if not resolved to the satisfaction of VK&amp;Co., would have caused
VK&amp;Co. to make reference to the subject matter of the disagreements in connection with
its reports. The report of VK&amp;Co. for fiscal year ended June 30, 2004 and 2003 indicated the
following: </FONT></P>

<!-- MARKER FORMAT-SHEET="Para Large Indent" FSL="Default" -->
<P><FONT FACE="Times New Roman, Times, Serif" SIZE=2>&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&#147;The
accompanying financial statements have been prepared assuming that the Company will
continue as a going concern. As discuss in Note 1 to the financial statements, the
Company&#146;s current liabilities exceeded its current assets as of June 30, 2004 and 2003. This
raises substantial doubt about the Company&#146;s ability to continue as a going concern.
Management&#146;s plans regarding these matters also are described in Note 1. The
financial statements do not include any adjustments that might result from the outcome of
this uncertainty.&#148; </FONT></P>

<!-- MARKER FORMAT-SHEET="Para Large Indent" FSL="Default" -->
<P><FONT FACE="Times New Roman, Times, Serif" SIZE=2>&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Except
as described in the immediately preceding paragraph, the report of VK&amp;Co. did not
contain an adverse opinion or a disclaimer of opinion, and was not qualified or modified
as to uncertainty, audit scope, or accounting principles. </FONT></P>

<!-- MARKER FORMAT-SHEET="Para Large Indent" FSL="Default" -->
<P><FONT FACE="Times New Roman, Times, Serif" SIZE=2>&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;In
response to the Company's request, VK&amp;Co. has furnished the Company with a letter
addressed to the Securities and Exchange Commission stating whether or not it agrees with
the above statements. A copy of the letter dated December 27, 2004 is attached as Exhibit
16 to this Form 8-K. </FONT></P>

<!-- MARKER FORMAT-SHEET="Para Large Indent" FSL="Default" -->
<P><FONT FACE="Times New Roman, Times, Serif" SIZE=2>&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;As
of the date of this Report, the Board of Directors of the Company has not approved nor
authorized the engagement of a new principal independent accountant for the Company. </FONT></P>
