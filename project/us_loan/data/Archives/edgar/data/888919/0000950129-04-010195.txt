<P align="left" style="font-size: 10pt"><B>Item&nbsp;3.02. Unregistered Sales of Equity Securities.</B>


<P align="left" style="font-size: 10pt">On December&nbsp;29, 2004, HCC Insurance Holdings, Inc. (the &#147;Company&#148;) issued $2,500,000 aggregate
principal amount of its 2.00% Convertible Exchange Notes due 2021 (&#147;New Notes&#148;) in exchange for a
like principal amount of the Company&#146;s 2.00% Convertible Notes due 2021 (&#147;Old Notes&#148;). The New
Notes are part of the same class of securities as the 2.00% Convertible Exchange Notes due 2021
issued by the Company pursuant to its tender offer which expired on November&nbsp;23, 2004.

<P align="left" style="font-size: 10pt">The New Notes were issued solely to existing security holders of the Company pursuant to an
exemption from registration under Section&nbsp;3(a)(9) of the Securities Act of 1933, as amended. The
Company did not pay or give, directly or indirectly, any commission or other remuneration,
including underwriting discounts, for solicitation of the exchange of the Old Notes for the New
Notes.

<P align="left" style="font-size: 10pt">The New Notes are convertible into common stock of the Company. The initial conversion rate,
subject to adjustment upon the occurrence of certain events, is 31.25 shares of common stock of the
Company per $1,000 principal amount of New Notes. Upon conversion of the New Notes, the conversion
consideration will be paid in cash up to the principal amount of the New Notes, and any excess over
the principal amount will be paid in common stock. The information under the heading &#147;Description
of New Notes &#151; Conversion Rights&#148; in the Company&#146;s Offering Memorandum dated October&nbsp;18, 2004,
filed with the Securities and Exchange Commission as Exhibit (a)(1)(i) to the Company&#146;s Schedule&nbsp;TO
dated October&nbsp;18, 2004, is incorporated herein by reference and more fully describes the terms of
conversion of the New Notes.
