<P align="left" style="font-size: 10pt"><B>ITEM
1.01.&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;ENTRY INTO A MATERIAL DEFINITIVE AGREEMENT.</B>



<P align="left" style="font-size: 10pt"><U>Second Amendment to Credit Agreement</U>



<P align="left" style="font-size: 10pt">&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;On December&nbsp;14, 2004, Dionex Corporation (the &#147;Company&#148;) executed an amendment (the
&#147;Amendment&#148;) to its existing Credit Agreement with Wells Fargo Bank, National Association (&#147;Wells
Fargo&#148;), dated November&nbsp;13, 2000, as amended (the &#147;Credit Agreement&#148;). The Amendment extends the
last day on which Wells Fargo will make advances to the Company under the line of credit provided
by the Credit Agreement from December&nbsp;31, 2004 to December&nbsp;31, 2006.


<P align="left" style="font-size: 10pt">&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;The summary and the description of the Amendment contained herein are qualified in their
entirety by reference to the full text of the Amendment filed herewith as Exhibit&nbsp;10.1 and
incorporated herein by reference.


<P align="left" style="font-size: 10pt"><U>2004 Equity Incentive Plan</U>



<P align="left" style="font-size: 10pt">&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;On October&nbsp;22, 2004, the Company&#146;s stockholders, upon the recommendation of the Board of
Directors, approved the Company&#146;s 2004 Equity Incentive Plan (the &#147;2004 Incentive Plan&#148;) as the
successor to, and continuation of, the Company&#146;s Stock Option Plan (the &#147;Option Plan&#148;) and the
Company&#146;s 1988 Directors&#146; Stock Option Plan (the &#147;Directors&#146; Plan&#148;). The 2004 Incentive Plan is a
single, comprehensive equity incentive plan that provides the Company with greater flexibility than
the predecessor plans to design and offer equity incentives in an environment where a number of
companies have moved from traditional option grants to other stock or stock-based awards, including
stock appreciation rights, stock purchase awards, stock bonus awards and stock unit awards.


<P align="left" style="font-size: 10pt">&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Following approval by the Company&#146;s stockholders of the 2004 Incentive Plan, all outstanding
options under the Option Plan and the Directors&#146; Plan now are subject to the 2004 Incentive Plan,
and no further options will be granted under the Option Plan or the Directors&#146; Plan. The maximum
number of shares of common stock available for issuance under the 2004 Incentive Plan is 3,520,119,
consisting solely of the number of shares of common stock that the Company estimates were carried
over from the Option Plan and the Directors&#146; Plan, including shares subject to outstanding options
under those predecessor plans.


<P align="left" style="font-size: 10pt">&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;A summary of the material features of the 2004 Incentive Plan is set forth in the Company&#146;s
Proxy Statement for the 2004 Annual Meeting of Stockholders (the &#147;Proxy Statement&#148;), filed with the
Securities and Exchange Commission (the &#147;SEC&#148;) on September&nbsp;17, 2004. The summary and the
description of the 2004 Incentive Plan contained herein and in the Proxy Statement are qualified in
their entirety by reference to the full text of the 2004 Incentive Plan, the form of agreement used
in connection with grants to non-employee directors and the form of agreement used in connection
with grants to persons other than non-employee directors filed as Exhibits 99.1, 99.2 and 99.3,
respectively, to the Company&#146;s Registration Statement on Form S-8, filed with the SEC on December
8, 2004.

<P align="center" style="font-size: 10pt">SECOND AMENDMENT TO CREDIT AGREEMENT



<P align="left" style="font-size: 10pt">&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;THIS SECOND AMENDMENT TO CREDIT AGREEMENT (this &#147;Amendment&#148;) is entered into as of
December&nbsp;1, 2004, by and between DIONEX CORPORATION, a California corporation (&#147;Borrower&#148;), and
WELLS FARGO BANK, NATIONAL ASSOCIATION (&#147;Bank&#148;).


<P align="center" style="font-size: 10pt"><U>RECITALS</U>



<P align="left" style="font-size: 10pt">&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;WHEREAS, Borrower is currently indebted to Bank pursuant to the terms and conditions of that
Credit Agreement between Borrower and Bank dated as of November&nbsp;13, 2000, as amended from time to
time (&#147;Credit Agreement&#148;).


<P align="left" style="font-size: 10pt">&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;WHEREAS, Bank and Borrower have agreed to certain changes in the terms and conditions set
forth in the Credit Agreement and have agreed to amend the Credit Agreement to reflect said
changes.


<P align="left" style="font-size: 10pt">&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;NOW, THEREFORE, for valuable consideration, the receipt and sufficiency of which are hereby
acknowledged, the parties hereto agree that the Credit Agreement shall be amended as follows:


<P align="left" style="font-size: 10pt">&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;1.&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Section&nbsp;1.1 (a)&nbsp;is hereby amended by deleting &#147;December&nbsp;31, 2004&#148; as the last day on which
Bank will make advances under the Line of Credit, and by substituting for said date &#147;December&nbsp;31,
2006,&#148; with such change to be effective upon the execution and delivery to Bank of a promissory
note substantially in the form of Exhibit&nbsp;A attached hereto (which promissory note shall replace
and be deemed the Line of Credit Note defined in and made pursuant to the Credit Agreement) and all
other contracts, instruments and documents required by Bank to evidence such change.


<P align="left" style="font-size: 10pt">&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;2.&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Section&nbsp;1.2. (a)&nbsp;is hereby deleted in its entirety, and the following substituted therefor:


<P align="left" style="font-size: 10pt">&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&#147;(a)&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;<U>Standby Letter of Credit</U>. Bank has issued or cause an affiliate to issue a
standby letter of credit for the account of Borrower and for the benefit of the former shareholders
of LC Packing (the &#147;Standby Letter of Credit&#148;) in the principal amount of Three Million Five
Hundred Thousand Dollars ($3,5000,000.00). The Standby Letter of Credit has an expiration date of
January&nbsp;31, 2005, and is subject to the additional terms of the Letter of Credit agreement,
application and any related documents required by Bank in connection with the issuance thereof (the
&#147;Letter of Credit Agreement&#148;). Subject to the terms and conditions of this Agreement, Bank hereby
confirms that the Standby Letter of Credit remains in full force and effect.&#148;
