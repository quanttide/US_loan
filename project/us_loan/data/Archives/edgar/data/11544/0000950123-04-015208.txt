<P align="center" style="font-size: 10pt"><B>W. R. BERKLEY CORPORATION ANNOUNCEMENT REGARDING<BR>
INDUSTRY REGULATORY INFORMATION REQUESTS</B>



<P align="left" style="font-size: 10pt">&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;<B>Greenwich, CT, December&nbsp;23, 2004 &#151; W. R. Berkley Corporation (NYSE: BER) </B>today announced
that, as previously disclosed, W. R. Berkley Corporation (the &#147;Company&#148;) had retained outside legal
counsel to conduct an internal investigation following the announcement by the New York Attorney
General&#146;s Office of its investigation into alleged anti-competitive activities in the insurance
industry. The Company commenced this internal investigation at its own initiative, not as a result
of any specific inquiry. Although the internal investigation is ongoing, outside legal counsel
have interviewed a number of employees and reviewed hundreds of thousands of pages of documents.


<P align="left" style="font-size: 10pt">&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Since commencing its internal investigation, the Company and certain of its operating units,
like many others in the insurance industry, have received information requests from various state
insurance regulators and other state authorities. These requests, for the most part, relate to
inquiries into inappropriate solicitation activities, producer compensation practices and the
underwriting of legal malpractice insurance. We are responding to each of these inquiries and are
cooperating with the applicable regulatory authorities. As a result of our investigation, a single
insurance operating unit reported certain limited instances of conduct which could be characterized
as involving inappropriate solicitation practices. To address these limited instances, the Company
has implemented certain additional internal procedures and is taking other corrective action.
