<H1 ALIGN=CENTER><FONT FACE="Times New Roman, Times, Serif" SIZE=2>Access Integrated
Technologies to Acquire Pavilion Movie Theater/Entertainment Complex in Brooklyn, New York&nbsp; </FONT></H1>

<!-- MARKER FORMAT-SHEET="Head Major Center Bold 1-TNR" FSL="Workstation" -->
<H1 ALIGN=CENTER><FONT FACE="Times New Roman, Times, Serif" SIZE=2><B>&#151;Will Serve as
Both Profit Center and Showcase for New Digital Cinema Technologies &#151;</B> </FONT></H1>


<!-- MARKER FORMAT-SHEET="Para Flush Lv 0-TNR" FSL="Workstation" -->
<P><FONT FACE="Times New Roman, Times, Serif" SIZE=2><B>MORRISTOWN, N.J. &#150; December
23, 2004 &#150; Access Integrated Technologies, Inc. (&#147;Access<I>IT</I>&#148;) (AMEX:
AIX)</B> today announced it has signed an agreement to acquire the businesses and assets
of the Pavilion Movie Theater/Entertainment Complex (the &#147;Pavilion&#148;) in the
Park Slope section of Brooklyn, New York.&nbsp; The purchase price will include
approximately $3.3 million in cash and a $1.7 million 5-year note.&nbsp; Closing is
subject to completion of due diligence and to obtaining financing satisfactory to <B>Access</B><I>IT</I>.  </FONT></P>

<!-- MARKER FORMAT-SHEET="Para Flush Lv 0-TNR" FSL="Workstation" -->
<P><FONT FACE="Times New Roman, Times, Serif" SIZE=2>Designed for an evening of total
entertainment, the Pavilion Complex includes the Living Room Caf&eacute;, a full-service
restaurant, a club, and an eight-screen multiplex.&nbsp; The popular showplace, housed in
a 1926 art deco building is also undergoing expansion with the addition of a ninth movie
screen.&nbsp; It now regularly attracts over 500,000 admissions annually from upscale Park
Slope and the surrounding communities.&nbsp; </FONT></P>


<!-- MARKER FORMAT-SHEET="Para Flush Lv 0-TNR" FSL="Workstation" -->
<P><FONT FACE="Times New Roman, Times, Serif" SIZE=2>The Pavilion will become an asset of
Access Digital Media, the division of <B>Access</B><I>IT</I> and a leading provider of
state-of-the-art digital film delivery and content management solutions and services,
employing technologies that are revolutionizing the motion picture exhibition
industry.&nbsp; Access Digital Media&#146;s &#147;Theater Command Center&#148; software
application provides in-theater storage and play-back capabilities to digitally-equipped
movie theaters around the United States.&nbsp; Continuing to operate as a fully functional
multiplex, the Pavilion will also become a showplace for <B>Access</B><I>IT</I> to
demonstrate its integrated digital cinema solutions to the movie entertainment industry. </FONT></P>


<!-- MARKER FORMAT-SHEET="Para Flush Lv 0-TNR" FSL="Default" -->
<P><FONT FACE="Times New Roman, Times, Serif" SIZE=2>&#147;In addition to being one of the
most complete and attractive single site entertainment centers in Metro New York offering
first run films, the Pavilion will afford <B>Access</B><I>IT</I> an ideal venue for
demonstrating to the public, the media, and other exhibitors the company&#146;s complete
suite of services, software, and satellite or fiber delivery systems.&nbsp; These systems
were used to deliver nine major movies in the last half of this year.&nbsp; It will be the
first multiplex installation of our &#145;Theater Command Center&#146; software, which
allows theater owners to manage and operate all equipment as well as to receive and
maintain a library of all movies, trailers and advertising showing in a multiplex&#148;
said Russell Wintner, President and COO of Access Digital Media.&nbsp; Wintner continued,
&#147;We will also deploy the Vista Point of Sales system, and our Exhibitor Management
System (EMS) for back-office, booking and accounting, both supported by
<B>Access</B><I>IT</I>&#146;s Hollywood Software business unit.&#148;</FONT></P>

<!-- MARKER FORMAT-SHEET="Para Flush Lv 0-TNR" FSL="Workstation" -->
<P><FONT FACE="Times New Roman, Times, Serif" SIZE=2>Bud Mayo, President and Chief
Executive Officer of <B>Access</B><I>IT</I> added:&#147;Besides adding another positive
cash flow generator to our business, we are tremendously excited about obtaining a fully
operational showcase at the Pavilion for our unique set of full-service digital cinema
solutions.&nbsp; It&#146;s another major step in our effort to build a company whose
various components work together synergistically to serve all the needs of the film
distribution and exhibition communities in the emerging digital era,&#148; he added.</FONT></P>


<!-- MARKER FORMAT-SHEET="Para Flush Lv 0-TNR" FSL="Workstation" -->
<P><FONT FACE="Times New Roman, Times, Serif" SIZE=2><B>Access</B><I>IT&#146;s</I> management
team is not new to film exhibition.&nbsp; Mr. Mayo founded the Clearview Cinema chain (&#147;Clearview&#148;),
