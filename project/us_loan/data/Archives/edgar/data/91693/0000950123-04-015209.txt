<P align="left" style="font-size: 10pt"><B>Item&nbsp;1.01. Entry into a Material Definitive Agreement</B>


<P align="left" style="font-size: 10pt">&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;<I>Intercompany Note</I>


<P align="left" style="font-size: 10pt">&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;On November&nbsp;24, 2004, Coinmach Service Corp. (&#147;CSC&#148;) completed its initial public offering
(the &#147;IPO&#148;) of income deposit securities (&#147;IDSs&#148;), and the shares of Class&nbsp;A common stock and 11.0%
senior secured notes underlying such IDSs (the &#147;IDS Notes&#148;), as well as its separate offering of
senior secured notes having the same terms as the IDS Notes (the &#147;Third Party Notes&#148;). The IDSs
and Third Party Notes were offered and sold pursuant to an effective registration statement on form
S-1 (File No.&nbsp;333-114421) filed with the Securities and Exchange Commission.


<P align="left" style="font-size: 10pt">&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Pursuant to a series of transactions related to the IPO (collectively, the &#147;Transactions&#148;),
Coinmach Corporation (the &#147;Company&#148;) became an indirect subsidiary of CSC. As part of the
Transactions, CSC used a portion of the IPO proceeds to make an intercompany loan to the Company in
an original principal amount of approximately $79.6&nbsp;million, which loan was evidenced by an
intercompany note. Interest under the intercompany note accrues at an annual rate of 10.95% and is
payable quarterly on March&nbsp;1, June&nbsp;1, September 1 and December 1 of each year, commencing on March
1, 2005, and is due and payable in full on December&nbsp;1, 2024.


<P align="left" style="font-size: 10pt">&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;The intercompany note is a senior unsecured obligation of the Company, ranks equally in right
of payment with all existing and future senior indebtedness of the Company and ranks senior in
right of payment to all existing and future subordinated indebtedness of the Company. The
Company&#146;s domestic restricted subsidiaries guarantee the intercompany note on a senior unsecured
basis.


<P align="left" style="font-size: 10pt">&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;On December&nbsp;21, 2004, the underwriters exercised a portion of their overallotment option for
578,199 IDSs. CSC used a portion of the proceeds from the underwriters partial exercise of the
overallotment option to loan an additional $2,130,085.12 to the Company, which loan was evidenced
by an increase in the intercompany note. As of the date hereof, the outstanding principal amount
under the intercompany note is $81,670,083.93.

<P align="center" style="font-size: 12pt"><B>COINMACH SERVICE CORP. ANNOUNCES OVERALLOTMENT OPTION<BR>
EXERCISE FOR 578,199 IDSs</B>



<P align="left" style="font-size: 10pt">&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;PLAINVIEW, N.Y., December&nbsp;17, 2004 &#151; Coinmach Service Corp. (the &#147;Company&#148;) (AMEX: &#147;DRY&#148;)
today announced that the underwriters of its initial public offering of income deposit securities
(&#147;IDSs&#148;) exercised a portion of their overallotment option to purchase additional IDSs, and will
purchase an additional 578,199 IDSs at the initial public offering price of $13.64 per IDS, less
the underwriters&#146; discount. Each IDS represents one share of Class&nbsp;A common stock and $6.14
principal amount of 11.0% senior secured notes due 2024.


<P align="left" style="font-size: 10pt">&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Coinmach Laundry Corporation, a wholly-owned subsidiary of the Company, will use the net
proceeds to redeem certain of its outstanding preferred stock from existing financial investors and
other current stockholders.


<P align="left" style="font-size: 10pt">&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Merrill Lynch &#038; Co. acted as sole book-running manager and Jefferies &#038; Company, Inc. and
Citigroup acted as joint lead managers of our initial public offering.


<P align="left" style="font-size: 10pt">&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Copies of the final prospectus relating to the offering may be obtained by contacting Merrill
Lynch &#038; Co., 4 World Financial Center, New York, NY 10080.


<P align="left" style="font-size: 10pt">&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;The Company, through its operating subsidiaries, is the leading supplier of outsourced laundry
equipment services for multi-family housing properties in North America. The Company&#146;s core
business involves leasing laundry rooms from building owners and property management companies,
installing and servicing laundry equipment, collecting revenues generated from laundry machines,
and operating retail laundromats.
