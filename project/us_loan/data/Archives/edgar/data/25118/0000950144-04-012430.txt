<P align="center" style="font-size: 10pt"><B>STATE REGULATORS APPROVE
TRANSACTIONS BETWEEN</B><BR>
<B>COUNTRY INSURANCE &#038; FINANCIAL SERVICES AND COTTON STATES INSURANCE</B>



<P align="left" style="font-size: 10pt">&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;ATLANTA (December&nbsp;22, 2004) The Illinois Division of Insurance has recently approved the
proposed merger of Cotton States Life Insurance Company with a subsidiary of COUNTRY Life Insurance
Company<SUP>&#174;</SUP>, and both Illinois and Connecticut have approved the affiliation of
Cotton States Mutual Insurance Company with the property/casualty insurance group of COUNTRY.
Regulatory approval in Georgia, where Cotton States is headquartered, was granted in November of
this year.


<P align="left" style="font-size: 10pt">&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;These regulatory approvals and the approval of Cotton States Life Insurance
Company (Nasdaq: CSLI) shareholders in October&nbsp;2004, are conditions to consummation of the
transactions. It is anticipated the transactions will close in early January, to be effective as of
January&nbsp;1, 2005.


<P align="left" style="font-size: 10pt">&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Upon consummation of the closing, Cotton States Life Insurance shareholders will
receive $20.25 cash for each share of outstanding common stock of Cotton States Life Insurance
Company, as stated in the Agreement and Plan of Merger. As a result, Cotton States Life Insurance
Company will become a privately held company.


<P align="left" style="font-size: 10pt">&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Further conditions of consummation of the
transactions include COUNTRY Life and the COUNTRY property/casualty insurance group retaining their
existing A.M. Best ratings of A&#043; (Superior) following closing, Cotton States Mutual obtaining an
A.M. Best rating of A&#043; (Superior) as a result of the transactions, as well as other customary
conditions to closing.

<P align="left" style="font-size: 10pt"><B>About COUNTRY</B>


<P align="left" style="font-size: 10pt">&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;COUNTRY<SUP>&#174;</SUP> Insurance &#038; Financial Services and its alliances serve about one million
households and businesses throughout the United States. Founded in 1925, the group now has nearly
2,000 exclusive agents in 14 Midwestern and Western states who offer a full range of
property/casualty, life and annuity products, as well as financial and estate planning services.
Through alliances, affiliations and relationships with independent agents, their geographic reach
spans across the United States. The COUNTRY property/casualty pool includes COUNTRY Mutual
Insurance Company, Bloomington, Ill.; Middlesex Mutual Assurance Company, Middletown, Conn.;
Holyoke Mutual Insurance Company in Salem, Salem, Mass.; and the MSI Insurance Companies, Arden
Hills, Minn. The COUNTRY insurance companies have nearly $1.5&nbsp;billion of property/casualty
premium, $530&nbsp;million in life premium and are consistently rated A&#043; by A.M. Best. For additional
information, visit www.countryfinancial.com.


<P align="left" style="font-size: 10pt"><B>About Cotton States</B>


<P align="left" style="font-size: 10pt">&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Cotton States Insurance is a group of companies that has been providing quality insurance
products to customers in the Southeast since 1941, with products for auto, home and life. The
Group&#146;s product portfolio is complemented with specialty products that are marketed through its two
brokerage operations. The Cotton States Insurance Group includes Cotton States Mutual Insurance
Company, Shield Insurance Company and Cotton States Life Insurance Company, including its two
subsidiaries Cotton States Marketing Resources, Inc. and CSI Brokerage Services, Inc. Cotton
States Life Insurance Company is traded on The NASDAQ National Market under the symbol CSLI. For
additional information, visit www.cottonstatesinsurance.com.
