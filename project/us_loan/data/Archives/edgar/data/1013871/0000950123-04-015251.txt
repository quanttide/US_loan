FD Disclosure.</B>

<P align="left" style="font-size: 10pt">On December&nbsp;27, 2004,
NRG Energy, Inc., or the Company, issued a press release announcing the closing of its sale
of $420&nbsp;million of its 4% convertible perpetual preferred stock.
A copy of the press release is furnished herewith as Exhibit&nbsp;99.1
and is incorporated herein by reference.
<!-- link2 "Item&nbsp;8.01. Other Events." -->

<P align="left" style="font-size: 10pt"><B>Item&nbsp;8.01. Other Events.</B>

<P align="left" style="font-size: 10pt">On December&nbsp;21, 2004, the Company entered into a Registration Rights Agreement for the benefit of
the holders of the Preferred Stock and the common stock issuable upon conversion thereof. Pursuant
to the terms of the Registration Rights Agreement, the Company has agreed that it will, on or prior
to April&nbsp;19, 2005, file a shelf registration statement with the
Securities and Exchange Commission covering resales of the Preferred Stock and the common stock
issuable upon conversion thereof. The Company has also agreed to use commercially reasonable
efforts to cause the shelf registration statement to be declared effective under the Securities Act
of 1933, as amended, no later than July&nbsp;18, 2005. In the event that the Company
fails to comply with certain of its obligations under the Registration Rights Agreement, the
Company could become obligated to pay liquidated damages to holders of the Preferred Stock and to
holders of any shares of common stock issued upon conversion of the Preferred Stock.


<P align="left" style="font-size: 10pt">A copy of the Registration Rights Agreement is attached hereto as Exhibit&nbsp;4.1.

<P align="center" style="font-size: 10pt"><B>NRG Successfully Completes Repurchase of 13
Million Common Shares<BR>
and Issuance of $420 Million of Perpetual Preferred Stock</B>


<P align="left" style="font-size: 10pt"><B>PRINCETON, NJ;
December&nbsp;27, 2004&#151;</B>NRG Energy, Inc. (NYSE:NRG) today
announced the closing of its sale of $420&nbsp;million of
convertible perpetual preferred stock with a dividend coupon rate of
4%. This transaction enabled the Company
to repurchase 13&nbsp;million common shares from investment partnerships managed by MatlinPatterson
Global Advisors, LLC (MP)&nbsp;at a purchase price of $31.16 per share. The cash proceeds from
the preferred stock issuance are expected to be used to redeem a portion of the
Company&#146;s existing debt while the
13&nbsp;million shares were repurchased using existing cash balances as depicted in Table 1 below:

<P align="left" style="font-size: 10pt">&#147;We are pleased with the
expeditious timing and completion of these transactions and with
the far-reaching positive implications for our stakeholders,&#148; said David Crane, President and
Chief Executive Officer. &#147;By contracting our share base, we
increased our remaining shareholders&#146;
stake in the Company&#146;s earnings potential while, through the
partial redemption of our 8% notes, we will achieve a meaningful reduction in our highest cost
debt.&#148;


<P align="left" style="font-size: 10pt">Under certain conditions, shares of the preferred stock will be convertible into NRG common stock
at $40.00 per share, which is approximately a 24.5% premium to the NRG stock closing price on
December&nbsp;14, 2004. Holders of preferred stock will be entitled to receive cash dividends at the
rate of 4% per year, payable quarterly. After five years, the preferred stock is callable at par
plus accrued and unpaid dividends. Proceeds from the sale of the
preferred securities are expected to be used
to redeem certain of the Company&#146;s 8% high yield notes at 108% of par in early 2005.


<P align="left" style="font-size: 10pt">As previously announced, NRG&#146;s 13&nbsp;million share repurchase from MP reduced their share ownership in
NRG to less than 10&nbsp;percent from the prior 21.5&nbsp;percent. Even with this repurchase, MP remains the
Company&#146;s largest shareholder. In addition, MP&#146;s registration rights were terminated and the three
directors affiliated with MP have resigned from the Company&#146;s
Board. The
NRG Board&#146;s Governance and Nominating Committee has begun the process of identifying appropriate
independent directors to fill the three vacancies.
