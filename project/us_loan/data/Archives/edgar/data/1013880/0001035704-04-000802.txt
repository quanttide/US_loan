<P align="left" style="font-size: 10pt"><B>ITEM 4.02 NON-RELIANCE ON PREVIOUSLY ISSUED FINANCIAL STATEMENTS OR A RELATED AUDIT REPORT OR
COMPLETED INTERIM REVIEW</B>


<P align="left" style="font-size: 10pt">On December&nbsp;28, 2004, the Audit Committee of TeleTech Holdings, Inc. (the &#147;Company&#148;) met and agreed
with management&#146;s recommendation that the Company (i)&nbsp;restate its financial statements for the
years 2003, 2002 and 2001 in its Amendment No.&nbsp;1 to Form 10-K for the fiscal year ended December
31, 2003 and (ii)&nbsp;to file this Form 8-K as requested by the Securities and Exchange Commission
(&#147;SEC&#148;).

<P align="left" style="font-size: 10pt">This filing describes certain adjustments the Company is making in the course of restating certain
of its prior period financial statements. The Audit Committee of the Company has discussed the
matters disclosed in this Form 8-K with the Company&#146;s current
Independent Auditors.

<P align="left" style="font-size: 10pt">As disclosed in Item&nbsp;9A. Disclosure Controls and Procedures of the original filing of the Company&#146;s
Annual Report on Form 10-K for the fiscal year ended December&nbsp;31, 2003, management completed a
comprehensive review of its tax returns and tax related balance sheet accounts. The review resulted
in amendments to previously filed tax returns, principally to correct tax basis depreciation
expense, record a liability for estimated sales and use taxes for the Company&#146;s Database Management
and Consulting segment, and adjustments to reconcile certain tax-related balance sheet accounts. In
addition, the Company completed a comprehensive analysis of its procedures for reconciling all
other significant balance sheet accounts and identified adjustments to grants, bank accounts,
health insurance accruals and payroll tax accounts that related to prior periods over the last
several years. Management concluded that approximately $11.7&nbsp;million of those adjustments related
to prior periods. However, management, the Audit Committee and the
Company&#146;s Independent Auditors determined that the charge recorded during 2003 related to
such adjustments was immaterial both individually and in the aggregate on both a quantitative and
qualitative basis to the trends in the financial statements for the periods presented, the prior
periods affected and to a fair presentation of the Company&#146;s financial condition and results of
operations.

<P align="left" style="font-size: 10pt">On December&nbsp;16, 2004, the SEC communicated to the Company through a comment letter that the SEC was
unable to concur with the Company&#146;s determination that the 2003 accounting adjustments recorded are
immaterial to previously issued financial statements in 2003, 2002 and 2001. The SEC requested that
the Company restate its financial statements for those years.

<P align="left" style="font-size: 10pt">The Company intends to file a Form 10-K/A for the fiscal year ended December&nbsp;31, 2003, containing
audited financial statements for 2003 and 2002 and, unaudited financial statements for 2001, as the
2001 financial statements were audited by Arthur Andersen. The Company will adjust the beginning
balance of the 2001 retained earnings to reflect the impact of adjustments related to prior years.
The Company will file Forms 10-Q/A for the quarters ended March&nbsp;31, 2004, June&nbsp;30, 2004 and
September&nbsp;30, 2004 to restate the prior years&#146; financial statements for comparability purposes.
