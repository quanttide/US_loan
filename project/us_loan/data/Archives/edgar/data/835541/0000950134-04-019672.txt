<P align="left" style="font-size: 10pt"><B>ITEM 2.02 Results of Operations and Financial Condition</B>


<P align="left" style="font-size: 10pt">On December&nbsp;21, 2004, Solectron Corporation announced the Company&#146;s results of operations for its
fiscal quarter ended November&nbsp;30, 2004. A copy of the Company&#146;s press release announcing such
results dated December&nbsp;21, 2004 is attached hereto as Exhibit&nbsp;99.1. This Form 8-K and the attached
exhibit are furnished to, but not filed with, the Securities and Exchange Commission (&#147;SEC&#148;) and
shall not be deemed to be incorporated by reference into any of the Company&#146;s filings with the SEC
under the Securities Act of 1933.

<P align="left" style="font-size: 10pt">Solectron includes the use of a non-GAAP financial measure in the attached exhibit. In accordance
with Item&nbsp;10(e)(i) of Regulation&nbsp;S-K, Solectron is required to provide a statement disclosing the
reasons why management believes that presentation of non-GAAP financial measures provides useful
information to investors regarding the Company&#146;s results of operations.

<P align="left" style="font-size: 10pt">Solectron management evaluates and makes operating decisions using various operating measures.
These measures are generally based on the revenues and certain costs of its operations, such as
cost of goods sold and selling, general and administrative expenses. One such measure is non-GAAP
net income (loss), which is a non-GAAP financial measure under Section&nbsp;101 of Regulation&nbsp;G under
the Securities Exchange Act of 1934, as amended. This measure consists of GAAP net income (loss)
from continuing operations excluding as applicable, restructuring charges (severance and benefits,
excess facilities and asset-related charges), investment related losses (gains), impairment charges
for goodwill and intangible assets, and losses (gains)&nbsp;on the extinguishment of debt. Non-GAAP net
income (loss)&nbsp;is adjusted by the amount of additional taxes or tax benefit that the company would
accrue if it used non-GAAP results instead of GAAP results to calculate the company&#146;s tax
liability.

<P align="left" style="font-size: 10pt">Management believes it is useful in measuring Solectron&#146;s operations to exclude restructuring
costs. Solectron has dramatically reduced headcount and facilities over the past three years. As
a result, in 2002, 2003 and 2004, Solectron&#146;s GAAP statements of operations have included
significant charges related to such restructurings. Solectron believes that in measuring its
operations it is useful to exclude restructuring charges as these costs are not part of the
company&#146;s direct cost of on-going operations. Furthermore, management believes investment related
losses (gains), impairment charges for goodwill and intangible assets, and losses (gains)&nbsp;on the
extinguishment of debt are infrequent events which make the results less comparable between
reporting periods.

<P align="left" style="font-size: 10pt">Management believes that non-GAAP net income (loss)&nbsp;provides useful supplemental information to
management and investors regarding the performance of the company&#146;s business operations and
facilitates comparisons to our historical operating results. Management also uses this information
internally for forecasting and budgeting. Non-GAAP financial measures should not be considered as
a substitute for measures of financial performance prepared in accordance with GAAP. Investors and
potential investors are encouraged to review the reconciliation of non-GAAP financial measures
contained within the attached press release with their most directly comparable GAAP financial
results.

<P align="center" style="font-size: 10pt"><B>Solectron Announces First-Quarter Results</B>



<P align="left" style="font-size: 10pt">For Immediate Release: Dec. 21, 2004


<P align="left" style="font-size: 10pt">MILPITAS, Calif. &#151; Solectron Corporation (NYSE:SLR), a leading provider of electronics
manufacturing and integrated supply chain services, today reported sales of $2.7&nbsp;billion in the
first quarter of fiscal 2005. Sales in the first quarter of fiscal 2004 were $2.7&nbsp;billion and
sales in the fourth quarter of fiscal 2004 were $3.0&nbsp;billion.

<P align="left" style="font-size: 10pt">The company reported GAAP income from continuing operations in the first quarter of $47&nbsp;million, or
5 cents per share, compared with a GAAP loss from continuing operations of $52&nbsp;million, or 6 cents
per share, in the first quarter of last year. Excluding $2&nbsp;million of charges related to
adjustments of previously announced restructuring, Solectron had non-GAAP net income from
continuing operations of $49&nbsp;million, or 5 cents per share.

<P align="left" style="font-size: 10pt">The company reported lower revenues from the previous quarter primarily
due to greater than expected weakness in the consumer
market for set-top boxes and 3-G wireless handsets, as well as in the semiconductor capital
equipment sector of the industrial market. In addition, the company saw lower demand in the
networking market.

<P align="left" style="font-size: 10pt">&#147;Despite lower sales, I am very pleased that earnings per share
improved to five cents, which was the midpoint of our guidance,&#148; said Mike Cannon, president and
chief executive officer. &#147;Our focus on profitability delivered a 40
basis-point improvement in gross margins from the prior quarter and a reduction in operating
expenses to $96&nbsp;million. In addition, we generated strong cash flow from operations of
approximately $190&nbsp;million. Looking
forward, we expect revenue growth in the second half of the year, driven by the expected ramp of
recent wins and improved demand.&#148;

<P align="left" style="font-size: 10pt"><B>Quarterly Highlights</B>


<P align="left" style="font-size: 10pt">The company made further improvements in working capital during the quarter with a reduction in
accounts receivable of $120&nbsp;million and a reduction in inventory of $52&nbsp;million. Inventory turns
were 7.1 and the company&#146;s cash conversion cycle was
51&nbsp;days. Capital expenditures were $32&nbsp;million,
depreciation and amortization were $49&nbsp;million, and return on invested capital
improved to 10.5&nbsp;percent.


<P align="left" style="font-size: 10pt"><B>Fiscal Second-Quarter 2005 Guidance</B>


<P align="left" style="font-size: 10pt">Fiscal second-quarter guidance is for sales of $2.65&nbsp;billion to $2.8&nbsp;billion and for non-GAAP EPS
from continuing operations to range from 4 cents to 6 cents, on a fully diluted basis.
